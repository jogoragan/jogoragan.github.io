Description

The pelvic floor muscles are an important part of the body that help support the bladder, uterus, and rectum. Weak pelvic floor muscles can lead to incontinence, pelvic organ prolapse, and even sexual dysfunction. Pelvic floor exercises, also known as Kegels, are often recommended for strengthening these muscles. However, planks can also be used to improve pelvic floor strength.

Planks are a popular exercise that work the core muscles, including the transversus abdominis, rectus abdominis, and obliques. These muscles are also connected to the pelvic floor muscles, which means that incorporating planks into your workout routine can help strengthen your pelvic floor as well.

How to Strengthen Your Pelvic Floor with Planks

Before attempting to use planks to strengthen your pelvic floor, it's important to make sure you are doing them correctly. Incorrect form can lead to injury and will not effectively work the targeted muscles.

Proper Form for Planks

To perform a plank:

  1. Start in a push-up position with your hands shoulder-width apart and your arms straight.
  2. Engage your core muscles and bring your body into a straight line from your head to your toes.
  3. Hold this position for 30 seconds to one minute, or as long as you can maintain proper form.
  4. Lower your body back down to the starting position.

Adding Pelvic Floor Engagement

To engage your pelvic floor muscles while doing a plank:

  1. Start in a push-up position as described above.
  2. Engage your core muscles and bring your body into a straight line from your head to your toes.
  3. Squeeze your pelvic floor muscles as if you are trying to stop the flow of urine.
  4. Hold this position for 30 seconds to one minute, or as long as you can maintain proper form.
  5. Lower your body back down to the starting position.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to incorporating planks into your workout routine to strengthen your pelvic floor:

  1. Warm up with some light cardio, such as walking or jogging, for five to ten minutes.
  2. Do two to three sets of planks, starting with 30 seconds and gradually working up to one minute per set.
  3. Try to engage your pelvic floor muscles while holding the plank position.
  4. Rest for 30 seconds to one minute between sets.
  5. Cool down with some stretching, holding each stretch for 10 to 15 seconds.

Tips for Effective Pelvic Floor Workouts

Here are some tips for getting the most out of your pelvic floor workouts:

  • Start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.
  • Focus on proper form to avoid injury and ensure effective muscle engagement.
  • Combine planks with other pelvic floor exercises, such as Kegels, for a more well-rounded workout.
  • Be consistent with your workouts, aiming for two to three sessions per week.

The Solution for Weak Pelvic Floor Muscles

If you are experiencing symptoms of weak pelvic floor muscles, such as incontinence or pelvic organ prolapse, it's important to seek medical advice. In addition to exercises like planks, there are other treatment options available, such as pelvic floor physical therapy or surgery.

FAQs

Can planks really help strengthen my pelvic floor?

Yes, planks can help strengthen your pelvic floor muscles by engaging the same core muscles that are connected to the pelvic floor.

How often should I do pelvic floor exercises?

For best results, aim to do pelvic floor exercises two to three times per week.

Can pelvic floor exercises help with incontinence?

Yes, pelvic floor exercises can help improve symptoms of incontinence by strengthening the muscles that support the bladder.

Pros and Cons of Planks for Pelvic Floor

Pros:

  • Planks are a popular exercise that can easily be incorporated into your workout routine.
  • By engaging the same core muscles as the pelvic floor, planks can help strengthen your pelvic floor muscles.

Cons:

  • Planks can be difficult for beginners or those with weak core muscles.
  • Incorrect form can lead to injury and ineffective muscle engagement.

In conclusion, planks can be an effective way to strengthen your pelvic floor muscles and improve overall core strength. By following proper form and incorporating pelvic floor engagement, you can make the most out of this popular exercise. However, it's important to seek medical advice if you are experiencing symptoms of weak pelvic floor muscles.
