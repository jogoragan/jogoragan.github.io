Morning Meeting Ideas For Students

As a teacher, you know that a morning meeting sets the tone for the rest of the day. It’s a time to connect with your students, build community, and set expectations. But coming up with fresh ideas every day can be a challenge. That’s why we’ve compiled a list of engaging and fun morning meeting ideas for students that will keep them excited about coming to class every day.

Table of Contents

  • Greeting
  • Sharing
  • Activity
  • Challenge
  • Reflection

Greeting

The greeting is the perfect way to start your morning meeting. It’s a chance for your students to connect with each other and with you. Here are some ideas:

Morning Meeting Idea 1: Handshake Greetings

Have your students pair up and practice different handshakes. Then have them switch partners and try a different handshake. This is a fun way to get your students moving and interacting with each other.

Morning Meeting Idea 2: Question of the Day

Ask a different question every day and have your students answer it as they enter the classroom. This is a great way to get your students thinking and talking to each other. Some sample questions include:

  • What was the best thing that happened to you yesterday?
  • What’s your favorite book?
  • What’s one thing you’re looking forward to this week?

Sharing

Sharing is a chance for your students to talk about themselves and their lives outside of school. Here are some ideas:

Morning Meeting Idea 3: Show and Tell

Have your students bring in something from home to share with the class. It could be a toy, a book, or a picture. This is a great way to get your students talking and sharing with each other.

Morning Meeting Idea 4: Compliment Circle

Have your students sit in a circle and take turns giving compliments to the person next to them. This is a great way to build community and boost your students’ self-esteem.

Activity

The activity is a chance for your students to get up and move around. Here are some ideas:

Morning Meeting Idea 5: Brain Breaks

Take a quick break from learning and have your students do a quick dance or stretch. This is a great way to get your students energized and ready to learn.

Morning Meeting Idea 6: Charades

Have your students take turns acting out different animals, sports, or objects. This is a fun way to get your students moving and laughing.

Challenge

The challenge is a chance for your students to work together and solve a problem. Here are some ideas:

Morning Meeting Idea 7: Puzzle Challenge

Have your students work in groups to solve a puzzle. This is a great way to build teamwork and problem-solving skills.

Morning Meeting Idea 8: Minute to Win It

Have your students compete in different challenges like stacking cups or bouncing a ping pong ball into a cup. This is a fun way to build teamwork and competition skills.

Reflection

The reflection is a chance for your students to think about their learning and set goals for the day. Here are some ideas:

Morning Meeting Idea 9: Journaling

Have your students take a few minutes to write in their journals about what they learned the day before and what they hope to learn today.

Morning Meeting Idea 10: Goal Setting

Have your students set goals for the day and write them down. This is a great way to get your students thinking about their learning and taking ownership of their education.

Conclusion

Morning meetings are an important part of your day as a teacher. They set the tone for the rest of the day and help build community in your classroom. By using these morning meeting ideas for students, you can keep your students engaged and excited about coming to class every day.
