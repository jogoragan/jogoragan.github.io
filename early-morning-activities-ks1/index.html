Are you looking for fun and engaging activities to start your day off right in KS1? Look no further! In this article, we will explore some early morning activities that are perfect for young learners. From physical activities to educational games, these activities will help your child start their day off on the right foot.

Table of Contents

  1. Physical Activities
  2. Educational Games
  3. Art Projects
  4. Mindfulness Exercises
  5. Music and Dance

Physical Activities

Getting the blood flowing first thing in the morning is a great way to wake up the body and mind. Here are some physical activities to try with your child:

Yoga

Yoga is a great way to stretch and relax the body while also promoting mindfulness. There are many resources online that offer kid-friendly yoga routines, such as Cosmic Kids Yoga on YouTube.

Dance Party

Put on your child's favorite tunes and have a dance party to get their body moving and energy flowing. This is a fun way to start the day and can help boost mood and motivation.

Obstacle Course

Create a simple obstacle course in your backyard or living room using items like cones, hula hoops, and jump ropes. This is a great way to get your child moving and practicing gross motor skills.

Educational Games

Starting off the day with some educational games can help your child get excited about learning and set a positive tone for the day. Here are some ideas:

Sight Word Bingo

Create a bingo board with sight words your child is working on and have them mark off words as they hear them. This is a fun way to practice reading and recognition skills.

Math Games

There are many online resources for math games that are both fun and educational. Websites like MathPlayground.com offer a variety of games for different age levels.

Scavenger Hunt

Create a scavenger hunt with clues that involve reading and problem-solving. This is a fun way to get your child thinking and practicing important skills.

Art Projects

Starting off the day with some creativity can help your child feel inspired and energized for the day ahead. Here are some simple art projects to try:

Drawing Challenge

Give your child a prompt, such as "draw your dream house" or "draw your favorite animal," and have them create a drawing. This is a fun way to encourage creativity and self-expression.

Collage

Provide your child with magazines, scissors, and glue and have them create a collage based on a theme, such as "nature" or "favorite things." This is a great way to practice fine motor skills and creativity.

Playdough

Playing with playdough can be a calming and creative activity for young learners. Provide your child with different colors of playdough and tools like cookie cutters and rolling pins.

Mindfulness Exercises

Starting off the day with some mindfulness exercises can help your child feel calm and centered. Here are some simple exercises to try:

Breathing Exercises

Teach your child some simple breathing exercises, such as taking deep breaths and counting to ten. This can help them feel more relaxed and focused.

Gratitude Practice

Have your child think of three things they are grateful for and share them with you. This is a great way to cultivate a positive mindset and start the day off with gratitude.

Visualization

Guide your child through a simple visualization exercise, such as imagining a peaceful place like a beach or forest. This can help them feel more calm and centered.

Music and Dance

Starting off the day with some music and dance can help your child feel energized and motivated. Here are some ideas:

Sing-Along

Put on some music and have your child sing along to their favorite songs. This is a fun way to practice language skills and boost mood.

Musical Instruments

Provide your child with simple musical instruments like shakers or tambourines and have them play along to a song. This is a fun way to practice rhythm and coordination.

Dance Along

Put on a music video or dance tutorial and have your child follow along. This is a fun way to get the body moving and practice coordination.

In conclusion, starting off the day with engaging and fun activities can help your child feel motivated and excited about the day ahead. Whether it's physical activities, educational games, art projects, mindfulness exercises, or music and dance, there are many options to choose from. Try different activities with your child and see which ones they enjoy the most.
