Welcome to Music City, USA! Nashville is a vibrant city that is known for its rich history, world-class music scene, and southern hospitality. Whether you are a local or visiting for the first time, there are plenty of things to do in Nashville during the day. In this guide, we will explore some of the best activities and attractions that you can enjoy in Nashville for a relaxing day out.

Explore Nashville's Rich History

Nashville is a city steeped in history, and there are plenty of attractions that pay homage to its past. One of the must-visit sites is the Belle Meade Plantation, which was once a world-renowned thoroughbred horse farm. Visitors can take a guided tour of the mansion, explore the grounds, and learn about the history of the plantation.

The Tennessee State Museum is another great destination for history buffs. The museum features exhibits that showcase the state's rich history, from Native American artifacts to Civil War memorabilia. Admission to the museum is free, so it's a great option for budget-conscious travelers.

Experience Nashville's Music Scene

No visit to Nashville would be complete without experiencing the city's world-class music scene. One of the best ways to do this is by visiting the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum. The museum features exhibits that explore the history of country music, from its earliest roots to the present day.

If you're looking for live music, head to Broadway, where you can find plenty of bars and clubs that offer live performances throughout the day. Some of the most popular spots include Tootsie's Orchid Lounge and The Ryman Auditorium.

Enjoy the Great Outdoors

Nashville is home to plenty of outdoor activities that are perfect for a relaxing day out. One of the best options is Shelby Bottoms Nature Center and Park, which features over 1,000 acres of green space, walking trails, and scenic views of the Cumberland River.

Another great option is Nashville Zoo, which is home to over 2,000 animals from around the world. Visitors can explore the exhibits, watch animal feedings, and even take a ride on the zoo's carousel.

Indulge in Nashville's Food Scene

Nashville is known for its mouthwatering food scene, and there are plenty of options for foodies to enjoy during the day. One of the best spots is Hattie B's Hot Chicken, which is famous for its spicy fried chicken. Another popular option is Biscuit Love, where you can indulge in southern-style biscuits and gravy.

If you're looking for a unique dining experience, head to The Waiting Room, where you can enjoy a meal in a vintage Airstream trailer. The restaurant offers a rotating menu of seasonal dishes, and the outdoor seating area is perfect for a sunny day.

Shop Till You Drop

Nashville is home to plenty of shopping destinations that are perfect for a day out. One of the best options is The Gulch, a trendy neighborhood that is home to plenty of boutiques, restaurants, and bars. Another great option is OneC1TY, a mixed-use development that features a variety of shops and restaurants.

If you're looking for a more traditional shopping experience, head to The Mall at Green Hills, which features over 100 stores, including high-end brands like Louis Vuitton and Tiffany & Co.

Discover Nashville's Art Scene

Nashville is home to a thriving art scene, and there are plenty of galleries and museums that showcase the city's creative side. One of the best options is the Frist Art Museum, which features a rotating collection of contemporary and classic art exhibits.

Another great option is the Cheekwood Estate & Gardens, which is home to a variety of art exhibits, as well as stunning botanical gardens. Visitors can explore the grounds, take a guided tour of the mansion, and even participate in a variety of art classes and workshops.

Relax in the Park

Nashville is home to plenty of parks and green spaces that are perfect for a relaxing day out. One of the best options is Percy Warner Park, which features over 2,000 acres of green space, hiking trails, and scenic views of the city. Another great option is Centennial Park, which is home to the iconic Parthenon replica, as well as plenty of walking paths and picnic areas.

Visit a Local Brewery

Nashville is home to a thriving craft beer scene, and there are plenty of local breweries that are worth a visit. One of the best options is Yazoo Brewing Company, which offers a variety of year-round and seasonal beers. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery, sample some beers in the taproom, and even grab some souvenirs to take home.

Another great option is Bearded Iris Brewing, which is known for its hazy IPAs and creative beer names. The brewery offers a variety of beers on tap, as well as a small kitchen that serves up tasty snacks and sandwiches.

Tour a Distillery

If beer isn't your thing, Nashville is also home to plenty of local distilleries that offer tours and tastings. One of the best options is the Jack Daniel's Distillery, which is located about an hour outside of Nashville. Visitors can take a guided tour of the distillery, learn about the history of Jack Daniel's, and even sample some of the famous whiskey.

Another great option is the Nelson's Green Brier Distillery, which is located in downtown Nashville. The distillery offers tours that showcase the history of the Green Brier brand, as well as tastings of their award-winning spirits.
