What To Do Each Day In Stardew Valley

Written by Alvine Mar 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Stardew Valley is a popular farming simulation game where players can live a simple life in a rural town. The game can be overwhelming at first, especially for new players. However, with a little guidance, players can get into the rhythm of the game and enjoy their time in the town. In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on what to do each day in Stardew Valley.

Table of Contents

  1. Wake Up and Check for Weather Report
  2. Water Your Crops
  3. Feed Your Animals
  4. Go Mining or Fishing
  5. Check for Quests and Deliveries
  6. Upgrade Your Tools
  7. Socialize with the Villagers
  8. Attend Festivals and Events
  9. Go to Bed
  10. Conclusion

Wake Up and Check for Weather Report

Start your day by waking up and checking for the weather report on your TV. The weather report will help you plan your day accordingly. If it’s going to rain, you can spend your time indoors, crafting, or socializing with the villagers. If it’s going to be sunny, you can spend your time outside, watering your crops or fishing.

Water Your Crops

After checking the weather report, head outside and water your crops. Watering your crops is crucial, as it keeps them healthy and helps them grow faster. Make sure to use your watering can to water each plant individually.

Feed Your Animals

If you have any animals on your farm, such as chickens or cows, make sure to feed them. Feeding your animals is essential, as it keeps them happy and healthy. You can also collect their eggs or milk, which you can sell or use to make other products.

Go Mining or Fishing

After taking care of your farm animals and crops, you can spend your time mining or fishing. Mining is an excellent way to find valuable resources, such as gems and ores, which you can use to upgrade your tools. Fishing is also a great way to earn money and catch valuable fish, which you can sell or use to cook delicious meals.

Check for Quests and Deliveries

Make sure to check for quests and deliveries at the town’s notice board. Completing quests and deliveries can earn you rewards, such as money and friendship points with the villagers. It’s also a great way to explore the town and meet new people.

Upgrade Your Tools

As you progress in the game, you’ll need to upgrade your tools to make farming and mining easier. You can upgrade your tools by visiting the blacksmith in town and paying him with the required materials. Upgrading your tools will also allow you to access new areas of the game and find more valuable resources.

Socialize with the Villagers

Socializing with the villagers is an essential part of the game. It helps you form friendships and even romantic relationships. You can give gifts to the villagers to increase your friendship points and learn more about their likes and dislikes. You can also attend events and festivals to interact with the villagers and earn rewards.

Attend Festivals and Events

Stardew Valley has many festivals and events throughout the year. Attending these events is a great way to have fun and earn rewards. Some events, such as the Egg Festival and the Fall Festival, have mini-games that you can participate in to win prizes. Others, such as the Flower Dance and the Luau, are more social events where you can interact with the villagers.

Go to Bed

As the day comes to an end, make sure to go to bed on time. Going to bed on time will ensure that you wake up in the morning with full energy and can start your day all over again.

Conclusion

Following these steps will help you make the most out of your time in Stardew Valley. Remember to take your time and enjoy the simple life in the town. With patience and dedication, you can build a successful farm and form lasting friendships with the villagers.
