Welcome to the day after Thanksgiving in New York City! This is the perfect time of year to explore the city, enjoy the holiday festivities, and get a head start on your holiday shopping. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best things to do in NYC on the day after Thanksgiving. From ice skating to holiday markets, there's something for everyone.

1. Ice Skating in Central Park

Ice skating in Central Park is a classic New York City winter activity. The Wollman Rink and Lasker Rink both open on the day after Thanksgiving. The Wollman Rink offers beautiful views of the city skyline and is located near the Central Park Zoo. The Lasker Rink is located in the northern part of the park and offers a more secluded and peaceful setting. Both rinks offer skate rentals and lessons for all ages and skill levels.

2. Holiday Markets

The holiday markets in New York City are a must-see during the holiday season. The Union Square Holiday Market, Columbus Circle Holiday Market, and Bryant Park Winter Village all open on the day after Thanksgiving. These markets offer a wide variety of handmade gifts, artisanal food, and holiday decorations. You can easily spend a whole day browsing the stalls, sipping hot cocoa, and enjoying the festive atmosphere.

3. Museums

New York City has some of the best museums in the world, and the day after Thanksgiving is a great time to explore them. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, the American Museum of Natural History, and the Museum of Modern Art are all open on this day. You can avoid the crowds and enjoy the exhibits at your own pace. Plus, many museums offer special holiday programming and events.

4. Holiday Lights Tour

The day after Thanksgiving is the perfect time to take a holiday lights tour of the city. Many tour companies offer bus or walking tours of the best holiday lights displays in New York City. You'll see the Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree, the holiday window displays on Fifth Avenue, and the dazzling lights of Dyker Heights in Brooklyn. It's a magical way to kick off the holiday season.

5. Broadway Shows

If you're a theater lover, the day after Thanksgiving is a great time to see a Broadway show. Many shows offer matinee performances on this day, and you can often find discounted tickets. From classic musicals like "The Phantom of the Opera" to new hits like "Hamilton," there's a show for every taste.

6. Restaurants

New York City is famous for its food, and the day after Thanksgiving is a great time to indulge. Many restaurants offer special holiday menus or brunch buffets. You can try traditional Thanksgiving dishes like turkey and stuffing, or explore the city's diverse culinary offerings. Just be sure to make a reservation in advance, as many restaurants fill up quickly.

7. Sports Games

If you're a sports fan, the day after Thanksgiving is a great time to catch a game. The New York Knicks and Brooklyn Nets both have home games on this day. Or, you can check out a college football game at Yankee Stadium. It's a fun way to spend the day with friends and family.

8. Movie Theaters

If you're looking for a more low-key activity, the day after Thanksgiving is a great time to catch a movie. Many new releases come out around Thanksgiving, and the movie theaters are often less crowded than on the weekend. Plus, you can enjoy some popcorn and candy while you watch.

9. Spa Day

If you need some relaxation after the hustle and bustle of Thanksgiving, a spa day is the perfect solution. New York City has some of the best spas in the world, offering everything from massages to facials to hot tubs. Treat yourself to a day of pampering and self-care.

10. Nightlife

Finally, if you're looking for some nightlife on the day after Thanksgiving, New York City has plenty to offer. From rooftop bars to nightclubs to live music venues, there's something for every taste. Just be sure to pace yourself, as the holiday season is a marathon, not a sprint.

Conclusion

The day after Thanksgiving is a great time to explore New York City, enjoy the holiday festivities, and get a head start on your holiday shopping. From ice skating to holiday markets to Broadway shows, there's something for everyone. We hope this guide has given you some ideas for how to spend your day in the city. Happy holidays!
