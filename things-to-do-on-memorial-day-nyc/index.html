Things To Do On Memorial Day Nyc A Guide To The Best Activities

Memorial Day is an important holiday that honors the brave men and women who have served in the United States armed forces. In New York City, there are many ways to pay tribute to our fallen heroes and celebrate their sacrifice. Whether you're a local or a tourist, there are plenty of things to do on Memorial Day in NYC. Here are some of the best activities to consider.

Visit the Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum

The Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum is one of the top attractions in New York City, and it's a great place to visit on Memorial Day. The museum features exhibits and displays that celebrate the courage and sacrifices of our armed forces, and it's a great way to learn more about our country's military history. Visitors can explore the decks of the USS Intrepid, tour a submarine, and see vintage aircraft up close.

Attend the Memorial Day Parade

Every year, New York City hosts a Memorial Day Parade that honors the men and women who have served in the armed forces. The parade features marching bands, military units, and veterans' groups, and it's a great way to show your support for our troops. The parade begins at 11am and proceeds up Fifth Avenue from 26th to 46th Street.

Visit the 9/11 Memorial and Museum

The 9/11 Memorial and Museum is a powerful tribute to the victims of the September 11th attacks, many of whom were members of the armed forces. The museum features exhibits and displays that tell the story of the attacks and their aftermath, and it's a great way to pay your respects to those who lost their lives. Visitors can see artifacts and exhibits that commemorate the bravery of the first responders and the resilience of our city.

Take a Harbor Cruise

A harbor cruise is a great way to see the city from a different perspective and enjoy the beautiful scenery of New York Harbor. On Memorial Day, many cruise companies offer special tours that highlight the military history of the city and its waterways. You can see the Statue of Liberty, the Brooklyn Bridge, and other iconic landmarks while learning about their significance to our country's history.

Visit the American Museum of Natural History

The American Museum of Natural History is one of the world's best museums, and it's a great place to visit on Memorial Day. The museum features exhibits and displays that celebrate the diversity of life on our planet, including many animals that have been used by the military in the past. Visitors can see dioramas of wildlife from around the world, as well as exhibits that showcase the importance of conservation and environmentalism.

Explore Central Park

Central Park is one of New York City's most iconic landmarks, and it's a great place to spend Memorial Day with friends and family. The park offers a wide range of activities, from picnicking and sunbathing to hiking and boating. You can rent a bike and explore the park's many trails, or take a leisurely stroll through the gardens and meadows.

Watch a Baseball Game

Baseball is America's favorite pastime, and there's no better way to celebrate Memorial Day than by watching a game at Yankee Stadium or Citi Field. Both stadiums offer special discounts and promotions for military personnel and their families, and you can enjoy a day of hot dogs, beer, and cheering on your favorite team.

Enjoy a BBQ

Memorial Day is also the unofficial start of summer, and many people celebrate by hosting a BBQ or picnic with friends and family. You can grill up some burgers and hot dogs, enjoy some refreshing drinks, and soak up the sun in one of the city's many parks or beaches. Just remember to clean up after yourself and respect the environment.

Pay Your Respects at a Cemetery

Finally, one of the most meaningful things you can do on Memorial Day is to visit a cemetery or memorial site and pay your respects to those who have served our country. In New York City, there are many cemeteries and monuments that honor our fallen heroes, including the Soldiers' and Sailors' Memorial Arch in Brooklyn and the Vietnam Veterans Memorial Plaza in Manhattan.

Conclusion

Memorial Day is a time to remember and honor the brave men and women who have served our country, and there are many ways to do so in New York City. Whether you prefer to visit a museum, attend a parade, or simply enjoy a BBQ with friends and family, there's something for everyone on this important holiday. So get out there and celebrate the sacrifices of our armed forces!
