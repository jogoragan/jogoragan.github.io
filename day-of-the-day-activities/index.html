Decorations:

One of the most important parts of celebrating Day of the Dead is decorating your home or workplace. Traditional decorations include papel picado (tissue paper banners), marigold flowers, and sugar skulls. You can also add candles, incense, and photographs of loved ones who have passed away. Here are a few tips for creating a beautiful Day of the Dead display:

  • Use bright colors like orange, yellow, and pink
  • Incorporate traditional symbols like skeletons, skulls, and crosses
  • Arrange your decorations in a way that tells a story or honors specific loved ones

Setting up an Altar:

One of the most important parts of celebrating Day of the Dead is setting up an altar, or ofrenda, to honor loved ones who have passed away. Here's how to create your own Day of the Dead altar:

  • Choose a location for your altar, such as a table or shelf
  • Cover the surface with a bright tablecloth or papel picado
  • Add photographs or mementos of loved ones who have passed away
  • Add candles, incense, and flowers
  • Offer traditional foods and drinks, like pan de muerto (bread of the dead) and tequila or mezcal

Costumes and Makeup:

Many people choose to dress up in traditional Day of the Dead costumes and makeup. Here are a few tips to get started:

  • Paint your face like a skull, adding colorful designs or flowers

Joining the Parade:

Many cities and towns host Day of the Dead parades, where people dress up in costumes and march through the streets. If you're interested in joining a parade, check with your local community center or Mexican cultural organization to see if there's an event near you.

Candlelight Vigil:

At the end of the Day of the Dead celebration, many people participate in a candlelight vigil to honor loved ones who have passed away. You can light candles and say a prayer or share memories of those who have passed.
