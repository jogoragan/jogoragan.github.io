How Often Should You Do Cardio While Building Muscle

When it comes to building muscle, many people focus solely on lifting weights and neglect the importance of cardio. However, incorporating cardiovascular exercise into your workout routine can have numerous benefits for both your heart health and muscle building efforts. But how often should you do cardio while building muscle? In this article, we’ll explore the answer to this question and provide some tips for incorporating cardio into your muscle-building routine.

Benefits of Cardio While Building Muscle

Cardiovascular exercise, such as running, cycling, or swimming, can offer numerous benefits for those looking to build muscle. Here are just a few:

Improved Heart Health

Cardiovascular exercise is excellent for improving heart health, as it helps to strengthen the heart muscle and improve circulation throughout the body. By incorporating cardio into your workout routine, you’ll be doing your heart a favor!

Increase in Endurance

By incorporating cardio into your workout routine, you’ll also be improving your endurance. This means you’ll be able to lift weights for longer periods of time, which can help you build muscle more effectively.

Reduced Risk of Injury

By improving your cardiovascular health and endurance, you’ll also be reducing your risk of injury while lifting weights. This is because you’ll have more stamina and be less likely to experience fatigue or muscle strains.

How Often Should You Do Cardio While Building Muscle?

The answer to this question largely depends on your individual goals and fitness level. However, as a general rule of thumb, you should aim to incorporate cardiovascular exercise into your workout routine at least 2-3 times per week. This will help to improve your heart health, endurance, and overall fitness.

If you’re looking to build muscle mass more quickly, you may want to reduce your cardio sessions to 1-2 times per week. This will allow you to focus more on lifting weights and building muscle. However, it’s important to note that cardiovascular exercise can still be beneficial even if you’re primarily focused on building muscle.

Additionally, the type of cardio you do can also impact how often you should do it. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) workouts, for example, are typically shorter in duration and can be done more frequently than longer, steady-state cardio sessions.

Incorporating Cardio into Your Muscle-Building Routine

If you’re looking to incorporate cardio into your muscle-building routine, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Don’t Overdo It

While cardiovascular exercise can be beneficial, it’s important not to overdo it. Too much cardio can actually hinder your muscle building efforts by burning too many calories and potentially causing muscle loss. Be sure to strike a balance between cardio and weightlifting.

Mix Up Your Workouts

Doing the same cardio workout over and over again can get boring quickly. Mix up your routine by trying different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, or swimming. You can also try different HIIT workouts to keep things interesting.

Listen to Your Body

As with any workout routine, it’s important to listen to your body and adjust as needed. If you’re feeling fatigued or experiencing muscle soreness, it may be time to take a break from cardio and focus more on weightlifting.

FAQs

Is cardio necessary for building muscle?

No, cardio is not necessary for building muscle. However, it can offer numerous benefits for those looking to improve their overall fitness and health.

Can too much cardio hinder muscle growth?

Yes, too much cardio can potentially hinder muscle growth by burning too many calories and causing muscle loss. It’s important to strike a balance between cardio and weightlifting.

What is the best type of cardio for building muscle?

There is no one “best” type of cardio for building muscle. It’s important to mix up your routine and try different types of cardio to keep things interesting and challenge your body.

Conclusion

So, how often should you do cardio while building muscle? The answer largely depends on your individual goals and fitness level. However, incorporating cardiovascular exercise into your workout routine at least 2-3 times per week can offer numerous benefits for both your heart health and muscle building efforts. Just be sure to strike a balance between cardio and weightlifting, mix up your workouts, and listen to your body!
