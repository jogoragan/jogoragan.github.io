Christmas Day in Manhattan is one of the most magical times of the year. The city is filled with holiday cheer, and there are plenty of activities to partake in. In this article, we'll be exploring what to do on Christmas Day in Manhattan, including where to go, what to see, and how to make the most of your time in the city.

Ice Skating at Rockefeller Center

If you're looking for a classic Christmas activity, ice skating at Rockefeller Center is a must-do. The iconic rink is open on Christmas Day, and it's the perfect way to get into the holiday spirit.

See the Christmas Lights

The Christmas lights in Manhattan are a sight to behold. From the tree at Rockefeller Center to the decorations on Fifth Avenue, there are plenty of places to see the lights. If you're looking for a more immersive experience, consider taking a Christmas lights tour.

Visit the Christmas Markets

The Christmas markets in Manhattan are a shopper's paradise. From artisanal crafts to delicious snacks, there's something for everyone. Some of the best markets include the Union Square Holiday Market and the Columbus Circle Holiday Market.

Attend a Christmas Day Church Service

If you're looking for a more traditional Christmas activity, attending a church service is a great option. There are plenty of churches in Manhattan that hold services on Christmas Day, including St. Patrick's Cathedral and Trinity Church.

Take a Walk in Central Park

Central Park is beautiful year-round, but it's especially magical during the holiday season. Take a walk through the park on Christmas Day, and you'll be treated to stunning views of the city skyline and festive decorations.

Catch a Broadway Show

Broadway shows are a staple of Manhattan, and there's no better time to catch a show than on Christmas Day. Matinee performances are often available, so you can enjoy a show and still have time for other activities.

Enjoy a Festive Meal

There's no shortage of restaurants in Manhattan, and many of them offer special Christmas Day menus. From traditional holiday fare to international cuisine, you're sure to find a meal that will satisfy your cravings.

Watch the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade

While it's technically not on Christmas Day, the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade is a beloved holiday tradition in Manhattan. The parade is held each year on Thanksgiving Day, and it's a spectacle that's not to be missed.

Visit the American Museum of Natural History

The American Museum of Natural History is one of the most popular museums in Manhattan, and it's open on Christmas Day. Explore the exhibits and learn about everything from dinosaurs to outer space.

Explore the Metropolitan Museum of Art

The Metropolitan Museum of Art is another must-visit museum in Manhattan. It's open on Christmas Day, and it's home to an impressive collection of art from around the world.

Tips for Making the Most of Your Christmas Day in Manhattan

  • Plan ahead and make reservations for any activities or restaurants you want to visit.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking around the city.
  • Dress warmly, as temperatures can be chilly in December.
  • Consider purchasing a MetroCard for easy transportation around the city.
  • Check the hours of any attractions you want to visit, as some may have limited holiday hours.

FAQ

Q: Is anything open on Christmas Day in Manhattan?

A: Yes, many attractions and restaurants are open on Christmas Day. However, some may have limited hours, so it's important to check ahead of time.

Q: What is the weather like in Manhattan on Christmas Day?

A: Temperatures in Manhattan in December can be chilly, with an average high of around 40 degrees Fahrenheit.

Pros and Cons of Spending Christmas Day in Manhattan

Pros

  • There are plenty of festive activities to partake in.
  • The city is filled with holiday cheer.
  • You can experience classic holiday traditions, like ice skating and seeing the Christmas lights.

Cons

  • The city can be crowded and busy.
  • Some attractions may have limited holiday hours.
  • Temperatures can be chilly.

Overall, spending Christmas Day in Manhattan is a wonderful way to get into the holiday spirit. From ice skating at Rockefeller Center to exploring the city's museums, there's something for everyone. Just be sure to plan ahead and dress warmly, and you're sure to have a memorable holiday.
