Things To Do In Nyc On Presidents Day Weekend

Presidents Day is a federal holiday celebrated in honor of George Washington, the first President of the United States. This year, Presidents Day falls on February 20th, which means a long weekend for most people. If you're planning to spend Presidents Day weekend in New York City, there are plenty of things to do. In this article, we'll give you a list of activities to make the most of your weekend in the Big Apple.

1. Visit the Museums

New York City has some of the best museums in the world. Spend your weekend exploring the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the American Museum of Natural History, or the Museum of Modern Art. Many museums offer special exhibits and events during Presidents Day weekend, so be sure to check their websites for details.

2. See a Broadway Show

Broadway shows are a must-see when you're in New York City. Book tickets in advance to see a hit show like Hamilton, Dear Evan Hansen, or The Lion King. If you're on a budget, try the TKTS booth in Times Square for discounted tickets to same-day shows.

3. Take a Stroll in Central Park

Central Park is a beautiful oasis in the middle of the city. Take a walk through the park, visit the Central Park Zoo, or rent a bike to explore the park's many paths and trails. If weather permits, pack a picnic and enjoy a peaceful afternoon in the park.

4. Explore the High Line

The High Line is a public park built on an elevated railway track above Manhattan's West Side. Walk along the park's 1.45 miles and enjoy stunning views of the city, art installations, and seasonal plantings. The park is open year-round, so it's a great activity for any season.

5. Visit the Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is an iconic symbol of American freedom and democracy. Take a boat tour to Liberty Island to see the statue up close and learn about its history at the museum. You can also visit Ellis Island and learn about the history of immigration in the United States.

6. Shop in SoHo

SoHo is one of New York City's trendiest neighborhoods, known for its high-end fashion boutiques, art galleries, and restaurants. Take a stroll down Broadway and explore the many shops and cafes. You're sure to find something unique to take home.

7. See the Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is an iconic landmark of New York City's skyline. Take the elevator to the observation deck on the 86th floor for breathtaking views of the city. For an extra special experience, visit at night to see the city lit up.

8. Visit the 9/11 Memorial and Museum

The 9/11 Memorial and Museum honors the victims of the September 11th terrorist attacks. The museum features exhibits, artifacts, and personal stories that commemorate the lives lost and the heroism of first responders. It's a solemn but important experience.

9. Try New Foods

New York City is known for its diverse food scene. Take advantage of your weekend to try new cuisines and restaurants. Whether you're in the mood for pizza, bagels, or international cuisine, you're sure to find something delicious in the city.

10. Go Ice Skating

Winter is the perfect time to go ice skating in New York City. Visit the iconic rink at Rockefeller Center, or try less crowded rinks like the Wollman Rink in Central Park or the Bryant Park rink. Bundle up and enjoy a classic winter activity.

Conclusion

As you can see, there are many things to do in New York City on Presidents Day weekend. From exploring museums to trying new foods, there's something for everyone. Make the most of your long weekend and enjoy all that the city has to offer.
