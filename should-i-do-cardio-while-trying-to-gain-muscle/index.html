Should I Do Cardio While Trying To Gain Muscle

Are you trying to gain muscle but wondering whether you should do cardio? This is a common question among people who want to build muscle mass. In this article, we’ll explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio while trying to gain muscle. We’ll also provide you with some tips on how to incorporate cardio into your muscle-building routine.

Benefits of Cardio while Trying to Gain Muscle

Cardiovascular exercise, also known as cardio, is any type of exercise that increases your heart rate and breathing rate. Doing cardio while trying to gain muscle has several benefits:

Burns Fat

Cardio helps burn fat, which can make your muscles more visible. When you do cardio, you burn calories, and if you burn more calories than you consume, you’ll lose fat. This can help you get rid of excess body fat and reveal your muscles.

Improves Cardiovascular Health

Cardiovascular exercise improves your heart health and lung function. It helps your heart pump blood more efficiently, which can improve your overall health and well-being. In addition, cardio can help reduce your risk of heart disease, stroke, and other chronic diseases.

Increases Endurance

Cardio improves your endurance, which can help you perform better during your muscle-building workouts. When you have better endurance, you can lift heavier weights and perform more reps, which can help you build more muscle mass.

Drawbacks of Cardio while Trying to Gain Muscle

While cardio has several benefits, there are also some drawbacks to doing cardio while trying to gain muscle:

Burns Calories

Cardio burns calories, which can make it harder to gain weight and build muscle. If you’re not eating enough calories to support your muscle-building goals, doing too much cardio can hinder your progress.

Stress on the Body

Cardio can be stressful on the body, especially if you do high-intensity cardio. This can lead to muscle breakdown and hinder your muscle-building progress. In addition, doing too much cardio can lead to overtraining, which can cause fatigue, injuries, and other health problems.

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Muscle-Building Routine

If you want to incorporate cardio into your muscle-building routine, there are several ways you can do it:

Do Cardio on Non-Workout Days

If you’re worried about cardio interfering with your muscle-building workouts, you can do cardio on non-workout days. This can help you burn fat and improve your cardiovascular health without interfering with your muscle-building progress.

Do Low-Intensity Cardio

If you’re worried about cardio burning too many calories, you can do low-intensity cardio. This includes activities like walking, jogging, and cycling at a steady pace. Low-intensity cardio burns fewer calories than high-intensity cardio, which can help you maintain your weight and build muscle.

Do Cardio after Your Workouts

If you want to do cardio after your muscle-building workouts, you can do so. This can help you burn fat and improve your cardiovascular health without interfering with your muscle-building progress. However, make sure you don’t do too much cardio, as this can lead to overtraining and hinder your muscle-building progress.

Tips for Doing Cardio while Trying to Gain Muscle

If you want to do cardio while trying to gain muscle, here are some tips to keep in mind:

Start Slow

If you’re new to cardio, start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. This can help prevent injuries and ensure that you’re able to stick to your routine.

Don’t Overdo It

Don’t do too much cardio, as this can hinder your muscle-building progress. Aim for 2-3 cardio sessions per week, and keep your workouts short and intense.

Eat Enough Calories

Eat enough calories to support your muscle-building goals. If you’re doing too much cardio and not eating enough, you can hinder your muscle-building progress.

FAQs

Q: Can I do cardio and build muscle at the same time?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and build muscle at the same time. However, make sure you’re eating enough calories to support your muscle-building goals, and don’t do too much cardio, as this can hinder your progress.

Q: How much cardio should I do while trying to gain muscle?

A: Aim for 2-3 cardio sessions per week, and keep your workouts short and intense.

Q: What type of cardio is best for building muscle?

A: Low-intensity cardio, such as walking and cycling, is best for building muscle. High-intensity cardio can hinder your muscle-building progress.

Conclusion

Cardio can be a valuable addition to your muscle-building routine. It can help you burn fat, improve your cardiovascular health, and increase your endurance. However, make sure you don’t do too much cardio, as this can hinder your muscle-building progress. Aim for 2-3 cardio sessions per week, and keep your workouts short and intense. And don’t forget to eat enough calories to support your muscle-building goals. With these tips, you can incorporate cardio into your muscle-building routine and achieve your fitness goals.
