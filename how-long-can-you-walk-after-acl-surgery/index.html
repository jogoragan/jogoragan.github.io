After undergoing ACL (anterior cruciate ligament) surgery, one of the most common questions patients ask is how long they have to wait before walking normally again. The answer to this question can vary depending on several factors, including the type of surgery, the extent of the injury, and the rehabilitation process. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how long you can walk after ACL surgery and what you can do to speed up your recovery.

How to Prepare for Walking After ACL Surgery

Before you start walking after ACL surgery, it is essential to prepare your body for the activity. Here are some things you can do to make the process easier:

Start Physical Therapy Early

Physical therapy is essential after ACL surgery as it helps to strengthen the muscles and increase flexibility. Your physical therapist will guide you through exercises that will help you prepare for walking after surgery.

Use Assistive Devices

Using assistive devices such as crutches or a walker can help you move around and take some pressure off your injured knee. Your doctor will prescribe the right assistive device for you.

Wear the Right Shoes

Wearing the right shoes is crucial for walking after ACL surgery. Choose shoes that provide support and stability, and that fit well. Avoid high heels and shoes with a narrow toe box.

Step-by-Step Guide on Walking After ACL Surgery

Walking after ACL surgery should be done gradually and under the guidance of your doctor or physical therapist. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start walking after ACL surgery:

Step 1: Use Assistive Devices

Start by using crutches or a walker to move around. This will take the pressure off your injured knee and allow your body to adjust to the weight-bearing activity gradually.

Step 2: Practice Weight-Bearing

Once you feel comfortable using the assistive device, you can start practicing weight-bearing on your injured leg. Start by putting a little weight on your injured leg and gradually increase the weight over time.

Step 3: Walk with a Slight Bend in Your Knee

When you start walking, make sure to keep a slight bend in your injured knee. This will reduce the pressure on your knee and make walking more comfortable.

Step 4: Increase Walking Time Gradually

As your knee heals and becomes stronger, you can increase the time you spend walking each day. Start with short walks and gradually increase the distance over time.

Tips for Walking After ACL Surgery

Here are some tips to help you walk more comfortably after ACL surgery:

Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to any pain or discomfort you feel while walking. If you experience any pain, stop and rest your knee.

Use Ice and Compression

Applying ice and compression to your knee after walking can help reduce swelling and pain. Use an ice pack or a cold compression wrap for 20 minutes at a time.

Stretch Before and After Walking

Stretching before and after walking can help improve flexibility and reduce the risk of injury. Your physical therapist can show you some stretches that are safe for you to perform.

Solutions to Common Walking Problems After ACL Surgery

Here are some solutions to common walking problems that you may experience after ACL surgery:

Swelling

If you experience swelling in your knee, elevate your leg and apply ice. You may also need to take anti-inflammatory medication as prescribed by your doctor.

Pain

If you experience pain while walking, talk to your doctor or physical therapist. They may recommend pain medication or adjust your rehabilitation program.

Stiffness

If you experience stiffness in your knee, perform stretching exercises to improve flexibility. You may also need to adjust your rehabilitation program to include more stretching exercises.

Frequently Asked Questions about Walking After ACL Surgery

Q: How long after ACL surgery can I start walking?

A: You can start walking with the help of assistive devices within a few days of surgery. However, you should follow your doctor's advice on when to start weight-bearing activity.

Q: How long does it take to walk normally after ACL surgery?

A: It can take several weeks to several months to walk normally after ACL surgery. The recovery time varies depending on the individual and the extent of the injury.

Q: Can I walk without a brace after ACL surgery?

A: Your doctor will advise you on when it is safe to walk without a brace after ACL surgery. In some cases, you may need to wear a brace for several weeks or months after surgery.

Pros and Cons of Walking After ACL Surgery

Pros

  • Walking is an essential part of the rehabilitation process after ACL surgery.
  • Walking can help improve strength, flexibility, and balance.
  • Walking can improve cardiovascular health and overall well-being.

Cons

  • Walking can be painful and uncomfortable after ACL surgery.
  • Walking too much or too soon after surgery can delay the healing process.
  • Walking can increase the risk of re-injury if not done properly.

Walking after ACL surgery is an important part of the rehabilitation process. It is essential to follow your doctor's advice and take things slow to avoid complications. With time and patience, you will be able to walk normally again.
