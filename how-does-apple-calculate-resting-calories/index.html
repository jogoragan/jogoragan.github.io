Resting calories are the calories you burn while you're at rest, and they're an important part of understanding your overall calorie burn. In this article, we'll take a closer look at how Apple calculates your resting calories and what you can do to optimize your results.

How To Check Your Resting Calories on Apple Devices

Checking your resting calories on Apple devices is simple. First, make sure you have the latest version of the Health app installed on your device. Once you've done that, follow these steps:

  1. Open the Health app on your device.
  2. Tap the "Browse" tab at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Scroll down to the "Health Categories" section and tap "Activity".
  4. Tap "Resting Energy" to see your resting calories.

It's important to note that your resting calories may vary depending on a number of factors, including your age, weight, height, and activity level. Apple uses a complex algorithm to calculate your resting calories, but there are a few key factors that play a role.

Step-by-Step Guide to Understanding Resting Calories

Now that you know how to check your resting calories on Apple devices, let's take a closer look at how Apple calculates these calories. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Determine Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body burns while you're at rest. This includes the energy your body needs to perform basic functions like breathing, circulating blood, and maintaining body temperature. Apple calculates your BMR using a complex algorithm that takes into account your age, weight, height, and gender.

Step 2: Adjust for Activity Level

Your resting calories can be further adjusted based on your activity level. If you're sedentary, your resting calories will be lower than if you're active. Apple uses data from your device's sensors to determine your activity level and adjust your resting calories accordingly.

Step 3: Adjust for Sleep

Your resting calories can also be adjusted based on your sleep patterns. If you're getting a good night's sleep, your resting calories may be higher than if you're not sleeping well. Apple uses data from your device's sensors to track your sleep and adjust your resting calories accordingly.

Tips for Optimizing Your Resting Calories

If you're looking to optimize your resting calories, there are a few things you can do:

Tip 1: Get Enough Sleep

As we mentioned earlier, getting enough sleep is important for optimizing your resting calories. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep per night to ensure your body is functioning at its best.

Tip 2: Stay Active

Staying active throughout the day can also help optimize your resting calories. Try to incorporate movement into your daily routine, such as taking a walk during your lunch break or doing some stretching while watching TV.

Tip 3: Maintain a Healthy Weight

Maintaining a healthy weight can also help optimize your resting calories. If you're overweight, your BMR may be lower than if you're at a healthy weight. Talk to your doctor or a registered dietitian for help with achieving and maintaining a healthy weight.

Solutions for Low Resting Calories

If you're concerned about your resting calories being too low, there are a few solutions you can try:

Solution 1: Increase Your Activity Level

If your resting calories are low, try increasing your activity level. This can help boost your metabolism and increase your overall calorie burn. Try taking a walk or doing some light stretching every day.

Solution 2: Get Enough Sleep

As we mentioned earlier, getting enough sleep is important for optimizing your resting calories. If you're not getting enough sleep, try adjusting your sleep habits to ensure you're getting at least 7-8 hours of sleep per night.

Solution 3: Talk to Your Doctor

If you're still concerned about your resting calories, talk to your doctor. They can help you determine if there's an underlying health condition that may be affecting your metabolism.
