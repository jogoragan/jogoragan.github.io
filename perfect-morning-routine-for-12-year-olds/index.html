As a 12 year old, it can be difficult to establish a morning routine that works for you. Between school, extracurricular activities, and socializing with friends, mornings can feel rushed and overwhelming. However, implementing a consistent morning routine can set the tone for a successful day. In this article, we will discuss the perfect morning routine for 12 year olds, including how to create a routine, step-by-step instructions, tips for success, and more.

Table of Contents

  • How to Create a Morning Routine
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Success
  • Solution to Common Problems

How to Create a Morning Routine

Before we dive into the step-by-step instructions, it's important to understand why a morning routine is beneficial. A morning routine can help improve productivity, reduce stress, and increase overall happiness. To create a morning routine, start by identifying your goals. Do you want to have more time to eat breakfast or exercise? Do you need to prioritize getting ready for school? Once you have identified your goals, create a list of tasks that will help you achieve them.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Wake up at the same time every day. This will help regulate your body's internal clock and make it easier to wake up in the morning.


2. Drink a glass of water. Hydration is important, especially after a long night's sleep.


3. Make your bed. This simple task can help you start the day with a sense of accomplishment.


4. Brush your teeth and wash your face. This will help you feel refreshed and ready to start the day.


5. Eat a healthy breakfast. This will provide you with the energy you need to tackle the day ahead.


6. Get dressed and pack your bag for school. This will ensure that you are prepared for the day ahead.


7. Spend some time on a hobby or activity that you enjoy. This can help reduce stress and improve overall happiness.


8. Leave for school on time. This will help reduce stress and ensure that you arrive at school ready to learn.


Tips for Success

1. Set your alarm clock across the room. This will force you to get out of bed to turn it off, making it less likely that you will hit snooze.


2. Lay out your clothes the night before. This will save you time in the morning and reduce stress.


3. Keep healthy breakfast options on hand. This will make it easier to eat a nutritious breakfast each morning.


4. Use a planner or calendar to stay organized. This will help you prioritize tasks and ensure that you don't forget anything important.


Solution to Common Problems

1. I don't have enough time in the morning.

Try waking up earlier or eliminating tasks that are not essential.


2. I have trouble getting out of bed.

Try setting your alarm clock across the room or using an app that requires you to solve a puzzle before turning off the alarm.


3. I don't have time to eat breakfast.

Try waking up earlier or preparing breakfast the night before.


FAQ

Q: How long should my morning routine be?

A: Your morning routine should be tailored to your individual needs and goals. However, a good rule of thumb is to allow yourself at least 30 minutes to an hour.


Q: What if I oversleep?

A: Don't panic! Simply adjust your routine accordingly and try to stay on track for the rest of the day.


Q: Can I include screen time in my morning routine?

A: While it's okay to check your phone or watch television for a few minutes, try to limit screen time in the morning as it can be a distraction and interfere with productivity.


Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Improved productivity
  • Reduced stress
  • Increased happiness
  • Established routine

Cons:

  • Requires discipline and consistency
  • May need to wake up earlier than usual
  • May take some time to establish a routine

By following these steps and tips, you can create the perfect morning routine for 12 year olds. Remember, establishing a routine takes time and discipline, but the benefits are well worth the effort. Good luck!
