How Much Exercise Should My 10 Month Old Puppy Get

As a new puppy owner, it’s important to know how much exercise your furry friend needs. By providing the right amount of physical activity, you can help prevent obesity, promote good behavior, and keep your puppy healthy. In this article, we’ll discuss how much exercise your 10-month-old puppy needs and provide some tips on how to keep them active and engaged.

Why Exercise is Important for Puppies

Exercise is crucial for your puppy’s overall health and wellbeing. It helps to build strong muscles, maintain a healthy weight, and improve cardiovascular health. Exercise also helps to prevent destructive behaviors like chewing, digging, and barking by giving your puppy an outlet for their energy.

Additionally, exercise provides mental stimulation and helps to prevent boredom. Puppies that are not provided with enough physical and mental stimulation are more likely to develop behavioral problems like anxiety, aggression, and depression.

How Much Exercise Does a 10 Month Old Puppy Need?

At 10 months old, your puppy should be getting around 60-90 minutes of exercise per day. This can come in the form of walks, playtime, and training sessions. However, it’s important to remember that every puppy is different and their exercise needs may vary depending on their breed, size, and energy level.

If your puppy is a high-energy breed like a Border Collie or a Labrador Retriever, they may require more exercise to keep them happy and healthy. On the other hand, if your puppy is a smaller breed like a Chihuahua or a Pomeranian, they may not need as much exercise.

How to Exercise Your 10 Month Old Puppy

There are many ways to exercise your puppy, including:

  • Walking - Taking your puppy for a walk is a great way to provide them with exercise and mental stimulation. Make sure to use a leash and collar or harness to keep them safe.
  • Playing - Playing fetch, tug-of-war, or other games with your puppy can be a fun way to exercise them and bond with them at the same time.
  • Training - Training sessions can provide your puppy with mental stimulation and exercise at the same time. Try teaching them new tricks or obedience commands.
  • Swimming - If your puppy enjoys the water, swimming can be a great low-impact form of exercise.

Tips for Keeping Your Puppy Active and Engaged

Here are a few tips for keeping your puppy active and engaged:

  • Vary your activities - Mix up your puppy’s exercise routine to keep them interested and engaged.
  • Use toys - Toys can provide your puppy with mental stimulation and exercise at the same time. Try puzzle toys or chew toys to keep them occupied.
  • Make it fun - Exercise doesn’t have to be a chore. Try to make it fun for both you and your puppy by incorporating games and positive reinforcement.

FAQ about Puppy Exercise

Q: Can I over-exercise my puppy?

A: Yes, it is possible to over-exercise your puppy. If your puppy is showing signs of exhaustion, like excessive panting or difficulty walking, it’s important to give them a break and let them rest.

Q: What should I do if my puppy doesn’t want to exercise?

A: If your puppy is not interested in exercising, it’s important to determine the cause. It could be a sign of a health problem, like arthritis or a respiratory issue, or they may simply be bored. Try to mix up their routine and provide them with new toys and activities to keep them engaged.

Q: Can I exercise my puppy indoors?

A: Yes, there are many indoor activities you can do with your puppy, like playing fetch in a hallway or teaching them new tricks in a spacious room. Just make sure to puppy-proof the area and remove any potential hazards.

Conclusion

Providing your 10-month-old puppy with the right amount of exercise is crucial for their overall health and wellbeing. By understanding their exercise needs and providing them with a variety of activities, you can help prevent obesity, promote good behavior, and keep your puppy happy and healthy for years to come.
