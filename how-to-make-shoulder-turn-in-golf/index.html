How To Make Shoulder Turn In Golf A Step By Step Guide

Golf is a sport that requires a lot of technique and practice. One of the most important aspects of a good golf swing is a proper shoulder turn. A good shoulder turn sets the foundation for the rest of the swing and allows you to generate more power and accuracy.

What is Shoulder Turn?

Shoulder turn is the rotation of your shoulders during the golf swing. This movement is essential for generating power and accuracy in your shots. A proper shoulder turn allows you to coil your upper body, creating potential energy that can be released during the downswing. This energy transfer results in a more powerful and accurate shot.

Why is Shoulder Turn Important?

Shoulder turn is important because it sets the foundation for the rest of the swing. A good shoulder turn allows you to generate more power and accuracy in your shot. It also helps you maintain your balance throughout the swing.

Without a proper shoulder turn, you may struggle to hit the ball straight or far enough. You may also experience pain or discomfort in your back or shoulders due to the lack of rotation in your upper body.

How to Make Shoulder Turn in Golf

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to make a proper shoulder turn:

Step 1: Take Your Stance

Start by taking your stance. Your feet should be shoulder-width apart with your toes pointing slightly outward. Your knees should be slightly bent, and your weight should be evenly distributed between your feet.

Step 2: Grip the Club

Grip the club with your dominant hand first, then place your other hand on the club. Your grip should be firm but not too tight.

Step 3: Address the Ball

Address the ball by positioning it in the middle of your stance. Your clubface should be square to the ball, and your body should be aligned with your target.

Step 4: Begin Your Swing

As you begin your swing, start by turning your shoulders away from your target. Your lead shoulder should rotate under your chin, and your weight should shift to your back foot.

Step 5: Complete Your Backswing

Continue turning your shoulders until your back is facing your target. Your lead arm should be straight, and your wrists should be hinged.

Step 6: Start Your Downswing

As you start your downswing, shift your weight back to your front foot and begin rotating your hips toward your target. Your shoulders should follow this movement, rotating back to their starting position.

Step 7: Follow Through

As you follow through with your swing, continue rotating your hips and shoulders toward your target. Your club should finish high and over your shoulder.

Tips for Making a Good Shoulder Turn

Here are some tips for making a good shoulder turn:

  • Use your core muscles to initiate the movement, not just your arms.
  • Maintain your spine angle throughout the swing.
  • Keep your head steady and avoid any unnecessary movement.
  • Practice with a mirror to ensure that you’re making a full turn.
  • Experiment with different shoulder turn angles to find what works best for you.

Common Questions About Shoulder Turn

What if I can’t make a full shoulder turn?

If you’re struggling to make a full shoulder turn, there could be several reasons why. It’s important to work with a golf instructor to identify the issue and develop a plan to address it. Some common reasons for a limited shoulder turn include poor flexibility, incorrect posture, or a lack of core strength.

Is it possible to make too much of a shoulder turn?

Yes, it is possible to make too much of a shoulder turn. This can lead to an over-the-top swing, which can result in a slice or other accuracy issues. It’s important to find the right balance between a full shoulder turn and maintaining proper swing mechanics.

Pros and Cons of Making a Good Shoulder Turn

Pros

  • Generates more power and accuracy in your shots
  • Helps maintain your balance throughout the swing
  • Reduces the risk of injury and pain in your back and shoulders

Cons

  • May take some time and practice to master
  • Can be difficult for those with limited flexibility or strength

Overall, making a good shoulder turn is essential for any golfer looking to improve their swing. By following these steps and tips, you can start developing a more powerful and accurate swing in no time!
