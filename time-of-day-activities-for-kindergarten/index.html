Time Of Day Activities For Kindergarten

If you're a kindergarten teacher or a parent of a kindergarten student, you know how important it is to keep young children engaged and active throughout the day. One way to do this is by incorporating time of day activities into your daily routine. In this article, we'll discuss some fun and educational activities to do with your kindergarteners throughout the day.

Table of Contents

  • Morning Activities
  • Midday Activities
  • Afternoon Activities

Morning Activities

Circle Time

Circle time is a great way to start the day and get your kindergarteners excited about learning. During circle time, you can sing songs, read books, and talk about the calendar and weather. This is also a good time to review any important information or announcements for the day.

Math Activities

Math is an important subject for kindergarteners to learn, and there are many fun and interactive activities you can do to help them develop their math skills. Some examples include counting games, shape and pattern recognition, and measuring activities.

Art Activities

Art is a great way to encourage creativity and self-expression in young children. During the morning, you can set up an art station with materials such as paint, markers, and paper. You can also provide themed art activities, such as creating a fall tree or a snowman.

Midday Activities

Outdoor Play

Getting outside and getting some fresh air and exercise is important for young children. During midday, you can take your kindergarteners outside for some free play time, or you can set up structured activities such as a nature scavenger hunt or a game of tag.

Story Time

Reading aloud to children is a great way to promote literacy and language development. During midday, you can gather your kindergarteners together for story time. You can read books related to the current theme or topic you are studying, or you can let the children choose their own books.

Music and Movement

Music and movement activities are a fun way to get children up and moving during the day. You can play music and encourage children to dance or move in different ways, or you can teach them simple songs and rhymes to sing and act out.

Afternoon Activities

Quiet Time

After a busy morning and midday, it's important to give children some quiet time to rest and recharge. During the afternoon, you can set up a quiet activity station with books, puzzles, or quiet games. You can also encourage children to take a nap or rest quietly on mats or blankets.

Science Activities

Science is an important subject for kindergarteners to learn, and there are many fun and engaging science activities you can do with them. Some examples include simple experiments with water or magnets, exploring nature and the outdoors, and learning about the human body.

Cooking Activities

Cooking activities are a great way to teach children about nutrition and healthy eating habits. During the afternoon, you can set up a cooking station and have children help prepare a healthy snack or meal. You can also tie cooking activities into current themes or topics you are studying, such as making apple sauce during a fall-themed unit.

Conclusion

By incorporating time of day activities into your kindergarten routine, you can keep children engaged, active, and excited about learning throughout the day. Whether you're focusing on math, art, science, or other subjects, there are many fun and educational activities you can do with your kindergarteners. Try out some of these ideas and see which ones work best for your class or child!
