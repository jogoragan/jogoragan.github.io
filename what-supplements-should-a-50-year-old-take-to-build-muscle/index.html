What Supplements Should A 50 Year Old Take To Build Muscle

As we age, our bodies go through natural changes that can make it more difficult to build and maintain muscle mass. But don’t worry, there are supplements that can help! In this article, we’ll discuss the best supplements for a 50-year-old looking to build muscle.

Table of Contents

  • Protein
  • Creatine
  • Beta-Alanine
  • HMB
  • Vitamin D
  • Omega-3s
  • FAQ

Protein

Protein is the building block of muscle, so it’s important to consume enough of it to support muscle growth. As we age, our bodies may not be as efficient at using protein, so it’s recommended that older adults consume more protein than younger adults.

One study found that older adults who consumed higher levels of protein were able to build and maintain more muscle mass than those who consumed lower levels of protein. Aim for at least 1.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day.

Creatine

Creatine is a popular supplement among athletes and bodybuilders for its ability to increase muscle mass and strength. It works by providing energy to the muscles during high-intensity exercise.

Research has shown that creatine supplementation can be particularly beneficial for older adults looking to build muscle. One study found that older adults who took creatine for 12 weeks were able to increase their muscle mass and strength.

Beta-Alanine

Beta-Alanine is an amino acid that can help increase muscle endurance and delay fatigue. It works by increasing the levels of carnosine in the muscles, which helps to buffer the muscles from the acid produced during exercise.

Research has shown that supplementing with beta-alanine can increase muscle mass and strength in older adults. One study found that older adults who took beta-alanine for 12 weeks were able to increase their muscle mass and strength.

HMB

HMB, or beta-hydroxy beta-methylbutyrate, is a metabolite of the amino acid leucine. It has been shown to help increase muscle mass and strength, particularly in older adults.

One study found that older adults who took HMB for 12 weeks were able to increase their muscle mass and strength. HMB may also have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce muscle damage and improve recovery.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is an important nutrient for overall health, but it’s also been shown to be important for muscle function. Research has shown that older adults with low levels of vitamin D may have decreased muscle mass and strength.

Supplementing with vitamin D may help improve muscle function in older adults. One study found that older adults who took vitamin D supplements for 12 months were able to improve their muscle strength and balance.

Omega-3s

Omega-3s are a type of fatty acid that are important for overall health, but they may also be beneficial for muscle function. Research has shown that supplementing with omega-3s may help improve muscle strength and function in older adults.

One study found that older adults who took omega-3 supplements for 6 months were able to increase their muscle strength and function. Omega-3s may also have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce muscle damage and improve recovery.

FAQ

What is the best supplement for building muscle?

There is no one “best” supplement for building muscle, as different supplements may work better for different people. However, protein, creatine, beta-alanine, HMB, vitamin D, and omega-3s are all supplements that have been shown to be effective for building muscle.

How much protein should a 50-year-old consume?

A 50-year-old should aim for at least 1.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day to support muscle growth.

Are supplements safe for older adults?

Most supplements are safe for older adults when taken as directed. However, it’s always a good idea to talk to a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

How long does it take to see results from supplements?

The time it takes to see results from supplements can vary depending on the individual and the supplement. Some supplements, like creatine, may start to show results in as little as a few weeks, while others may take several months to show noticeable changes.

Conclusion

If you’re a 50-year-old looking to build muscle, there are several supplements that can help. Protein, creatine, beta-alanine, HMB, vitamin D, and omega-3s are all supplements that have been shown to be effective for building muscle in older adults. As always, it’s important to talk to a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.
