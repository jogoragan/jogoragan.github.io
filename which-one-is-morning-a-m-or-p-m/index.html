When it comes to telling the time, one of the most important things to be clear on is whether it is morning or evening. This is where the terms A.M. and P.M. come in handy. However, for some people who are not familiar with these terms, it can cause some confusion. In this article, we will help you understand which one is morning: A.M. or P.M.

What is A.M.?

A.M. stands for ante meridiem, which is Latin for "before noon." This term is used to describe the time between midnight and noon. For example, if you wake up at 6:00 A.M., it means that you woke up during the morning hours.

What is P.M.?

P.M. stands for post meridiem, which is Latin for "afternoon." This term is used to describe the time between noon and midnight. For example, if you have dinner at 7:00 P.M., it means that you had your meal during the evening hours.

How to Remember A.M. and P.M.

One way to remember which one is morning is to associate A.M. with "ante meridiem" and "before noon." On the other hand, you can associate P.M. with "post meridiem" and "afternoon. Another way to remember is to use the acronym "Ate Morning" for A.M. and "Past Midnight" for P.M.
