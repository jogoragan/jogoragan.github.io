Description

Starting the day with a morning meeting in kindergarten is a great way to build a sense of community and establish a routine. However, it can be challenging to come up with engaging and age-appropriate activities that keep kids focused and excited for the day ahead. In this article, we'll share some easy morning meeting activities for kindergarten that are sure to be a hit.

How to

When planning your kindergarten morning meeting, it's important to keep in mind that young children have short attention spans and need lots of movement and interaction. Here are a few tips for creating a successful morning meeting:

  • Keep it short and sweet - aim for 10-15 minutes
  • Incorporate movement and singing to keep kids engaged
  • Use visuals and props to help children understand concepts
  • Encourage participation and give every child a chance to share

Step by Step

Here are five easy morning meeting activities for kindergarten:

1. Greeting

Start the day with a friendly greeting. Have each child say good morning to their neighbor or do a fun call-and-response greeting like "Good morning, sunshine!" "Good morning, flowers!"

2. Movement Song

Get kids up and moving with a fun song like "Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes" or "If You're Happy and You Know It."

3. Calendar Time

Use a calendar to practice counting, days of the week, and months of the year. Have a different child each day move the date and lead the calendar activity.

4. Show and Tell

Give each child a chance to share something special with the class. This could be a favorite toy, a drawing, or a story from the weekend.

5. Story Time

End the morning meeting with a read-aloud of a fun and engaging book.

Tips

  • Have a designated spot for morning meeting that is comfortable and free from distractions
  • Vary the activities each day to keep kids engaged and excited
  • Encourage children to ask questions and share their thoughts and opinions
  • Use positive reinforcement and praise to recognize good behavior and participation

FAQ

Q: How often should we have morning meetings?

A: Morning meetings are typically held every day to establish a routine and build a sense of community. However, you can adjust the frequency to fit your class's needs.

Q: What if a child doesn't want to participate?

A: Encourage participation without forcing it. Offer different options for sharing or participating, such as drawing a picture instead of speaking aloud.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Establishes a routine and sense of community
  • Encourages participation and social interaction
  • Introduces important concepts like counting and days of the week

Cons:

  • Can be challenging to keep kids engaged and focused
  • May not work for every classroom or teacher

Overall, morning meetings are a great way to start the day in kindergarten. With these easy activities and tips, you can create a fun and engaging morning meeting that sets your students up for success.
