How Many Calories In A White Bread Roll

White bread rolls are a popular food item in many households. They are often served as a side dish or used to make sandwiches. However, if you are watching your calorie intake, you may be wondering how many calories are in a white bread roll. In this article, we will explore the calorie content of these rolls, and provide tips on how to include them in your diet without going overboard on calories.

Calories in a White Bread Roll

A typical white bread roll contains around 120-150 calories, depending on its size and ingredients. However, this number can vary widely depending on the recipe used to make the rolls.

For example, a large white bread roll made with enriched flour and added sugar can contain up to 200 calories, while a smaller roll made with whole wheat flour and no added sugar may contain only 90 calories.

If you are trying to watch your calorie intake, it is important to pay attention to the serving size and ingredients of the white bread roll you are consuming.

How to Reduce Calories in a White Bread Roll

If you are looking to reduce the calorie content of your white bread rolls, there are several strategies you can try:

  • Use whole wheat flour instead of enriched flour
  • Avoid adding sugar or sweeteners
  • Use low-fat milk or water instead of full-fat milk
  • Use olive oil or another healthy oil instead of butter or margarine
  • Make smaller rolls to reduce portion size

By making these swaps, you can significantly reduce the calorie content of your white bread rolls without sacrificing taste or texture.

Step-by-Step: Making a Low-Calorie White Bread Roll

Here is a simple recipe for making a low-calorie white bread roll:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and honey. Let sit for 5-10 minutes, or until bubbly.
  3. Add the yeast mixture to the flour mixture and stir until well combined.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 5-10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down the dough and shape it into small rolls.
  7. Place the rolls on a greased baking sheet, brush with olive oil, and let rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Bake the rolls in a preheated oven at 375°F for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

By following this recipe, you can make low-calorie white bread rolls that are both delicious and nutritious.

Tips for Including White Bread Rolls in Your Diet

Here are some tips for including white bread rolls in your diet without going overboard on calories:

  • Use smaller rolls to reduce portion size
  • Pair white bread rolls with lean protein, such as grilled chicken or fish
  • Include plenty of vegetables in your meal to increase nutrient density
  • Avoid adding high-calorie condiments, such as butter or mayonnaise

By following these tips, you can enjoy white bread rolls as part of a balanced and healthy diet.

Solution: Healthier Alternatives to White Bread Rolls

If you are looking for healthier alternatives to white bread rolls, there are several options to consider:

  • Whole wheat rolls
  • Multigrain rolls
  • Sourdough rolls
  • Pita bread
  • Wraps made with whole wheat or spinach tortillas

These alternatives are often lower in calories and higher in nutrients than traditional white bread rolls.

FAQs

Q: Are white bread rolls bad for you?

A: White bread rolls are not inherently bad for you, but they are often high in calories and low in nutrients. If you are watching your calorie intake or trying to eat a more nutrient-dense diet, it is important to consume white bread rolls in moderation.

Q: Can I freeze white bread rolls?

A: Yes, you can freeze white bread rolls for up to 3 months. To thaw, simply leave them out at room temperature for a few hours or heat in the oven at 350°F for 10-15 minutes.

Pros and Cons of Eating White Bread Rolls

Pros:

  • Convenient and easy to make
  • Can be used in a variety of dishes, from sandwiches to side dishes
  • Can be made with a variety of ingredients to suit different tastes and preferences

Cons:

  • Often high in calories and low in nutrients
  • May contribute to weight gain and other health issues if consumed in excess
  • May contain added sugars and preservatives

Overall, white bread rolls can be a tasty and convenient addition to your diet, but it is important to consume them in moderation and choose healthier alternatives whenever possible.
