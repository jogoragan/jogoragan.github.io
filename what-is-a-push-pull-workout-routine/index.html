What Is A Push Pull Workout Routine

Written by Alvine Jan 22, 2023 · 3 min read

A push pull workout routine is a type of weightlifting program that divides exercises into two categories: pushing movements and pulling movements. This routine is designed to help individuals maximize their training efforts by targeting specific muscle groups on specific days. If you’re looking to build muscle, increase strength, or improve overall fitness, a push pull workout routine may be just what you need.

How to Do a Push Pull Workout Routine

Before starting a push pull workout routine, you should warm up with some light cardio and stretching exercises. Once you’re warmed up, you can begin your workout.

Step 1: Push Day

On push day, you’ll focus on exercises that involve pushing movements. This includes exercises like bench press, shoulder press, and tricep dips. You’ll want to do around 3-4 sets of each exercise, with 8-12 reps per set.

Step 2: Pull Day

On pull day, you’ll focus on exercises that involve pulling movements. This includes exercises like pull-ups, rows, and bicep curls. Again, you’ll want to do around 3-4 sets of each exercise, with 8-12 reps per set.

Step 3: Rest Day

After two days of intense training, it’s important to take a rest day to allow your muscles to recover.

Step 4: Repeat

After your rest day, you’ll start over with push day and continue the cycle. You can do this routine 3-4 times per week, depending on your fitness goals and level of experience.

Tips for a Successful Push Pull Workout Routine

To get the most out of your push pull workout routine, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Make sure you’re using proper form on all exercises to avoid injury.
  • Gradually increase the weight you’re lifting to challenge your muscles and avoid plateauing.
  • Don’t forget to stretch after your workout to prevent muscle soreness and tightness.
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with healthy foods to support muscle growth and recovery.

Pros and Cons of a Push Pull Workout Routine

Like any workout routine, there are pros and cons to a push pull workout routine. Here are a few to consider:

Pros

  • Targets specific muscle groups for optimal results.
  • Allows for more recovery time between workouts.
  • Can be customized to fit individual fitness goals.
  • Helps prevent overtraining and injury.

Cons

  • May not provide enough variety for some individuals.
  • Requires access to weightlifting equipment.
  • Can be challenging for beginners to learn proper form and technique.
  • May not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions or injuries.

FAQs

What is the difference between push and pull exercises?

Push exercises involve pushing movements, like pushing a weight away from your body. Pull exercises involve pulling movements, like pulling a weight towards your body.

How often should I do a push pull workout routine?

You can do a push pull workout routine 3-4 times per week, depending on your fitness goals and level of experience.

Can I do a push pull workout routine at home?

Yes, you can do a push pull workout routine at home if you have access to weightlifting equipment. You can also modify exercises to use bodyweight or resistance bands if you don’t have access to weights.

Is a push pull workout routine good for weight loss?

While a push pull workout routine is great for building muscle and increasing strength, it may not be the most effective workout for weight loss. To lose weight, you’ll need to combine a healthy diet with regular exercise that includes cardio and strength training.
