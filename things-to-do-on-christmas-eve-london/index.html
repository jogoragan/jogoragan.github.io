Christmas Eve in London is a magical time of the year. The city is alive with festive cheer as people rush to finish their last-minute shopping, enjoy the Christmas lights and decorations, and indulge in festive treats. If you're looking for things to do on Christmas Eve in London, we've got you covered. Here are our top picks for a relaxed festive day out in the city.

1. Visit Winter Wonderland

Winter Wonderland is one of London's most popular Christmas attractions. Located in Hyde Park, this winter wonderland features ice-skating, a giant observation wheel, festive markets, and a range of rides and attractions. It's the perfect place to get into the festive spirit and enjoy some family-friendly fun. If you're looking for things to do on Christmas Eve in London, Winter Wonderland is a must-visit.

2. Take a Christmas Lights Tour

London is famous for its Christmas lights, and there's no better way to see them than on a guided tour. There are plenty of tours available, from walking tours to bus tours, so you can choose the one that suits you best. You'll see some of the city's most iconic landmarks lit up in festive lights, including Oxford Street, Regent Street, and Covent Garden. It's a magical experience that's sure to get you in the festive mood.

3. Attend a Christmas Carol Service

Attending a Christmas carol service is a traditional way to celebrate Christmas Eve in London. There are plenty of churches and cathedrals across the city that offer carol services, including St. Paul's Cathedral, Westminster Abbey, and Southwark Cathedral. It's a beautiful way to celebrate the true meaning of Christmas and enjoy some wonderful music.

4. Go Ice Skating

Ice skating is a popular festive activity in London, and there are plenty of rinks to choose from. From the iconic rink at Somerset House to the beautiful rink at the Natural History Museum, there's something for everyone. Skating under the stars with the backdrop of the city skyline is a magical experience that's sure to make your Christmas Eve in London unforgettable.

5. Enjoy Festive Treats

No Christmas Eve in London is complete without indulging in some festive treats. From mince pies to mulled wine, there are plenty of delicious treats to enjoy. Visit one of the city's traditional markets, such as Borough Market or Camden Market, to sample some of the best festive food and drink on offer.

6. Relax at a Christmas Market

London is home to some of the best Christmas markets in the world, and there's no better way to get into the festive spirit than by visiting one. From the traditional German-style market at Southbank Centre to the magical market at Leicester Square, there's something for everyone. Enjoy some festive shopping, indulge in some delicious food and drink, and soak up the festive atmosphere.

7. Watch a Festive Film

There's nothing quite like settling down to watch a festive film on Christmas Eve in London. From classic Christmas films such as It's a Wonderful Life and Miracle on 34th Street to modern favourites such as Love Actually and Elf, there's a film to suit every taste. Visit one of the city's independent cinemas, such as the Prince Charles Cinema or the Electric Cinema, for a truly magical experience.

8. Visit a Museum or Gallery

If you're looking for a more relaxed way to spend Christmas Eve in London, why not visit one of the city's many museums or galleries? Many of them are open on Christmas Eve, so you can enjoy some culture and history while avoiding the crowds. The British Museum, the National Gallery, and the Tate Modern are all great options.

9. Attend a Christmas Eve Mass

Attending a Christmas Eve mass is a traditional way to celebrate Christmas in London. There are plenty of churches and cathedrals across the city that offer mass services, including St. Paul's Cathedral, Westminster Abbey, and Southwark Cathedral. It's a beautiful way to celebrate the true meaning of Christmas and enjoy some wonderful music.

10. Take a Festive Walk

If you're looking for a more low-key way to spend Christmas Eve in London, why not take a festive walk? From the beautiful parks to the iconic landmarks, there's plenty to see and do. Walk along the Thames, visit Trafalgar Square, or explore one of the city's many parks. It's a great way to enjoy the festive atmosphere and get some fresh air.

So there you have it, our top picks for things to do on Christmas Eve in London. Whether you're looking for family-friendly fun or a more low-key way to celebrate, there's something for everyone. So why not make this Christmas Eve in London one to remember?
