How Long Should You Go To Physical Therapy After Acl Surgery

Introduction

After undergoing ACL surgery, physical therapy is an essential part of the recovery process. It is recommended by doctors and physical therapists to ensure that the patient has a full recovery and is able to return to their daily activities. The question is, how long should you go to physical therapy after ACL surgery? This article will discuss the recommended duration of physical therapy, its pros and cons, and answer some frequently asked questions about it.

How Long Should You Go to Physical Therapy After ACL Surgery?

The duration of physical therapy after ACL surgery varies depending on several factors such as the patient's age, overall health, type of surgery, and the extent of the injury. Typically, the duration of physical therapy after ACL surgery lasts for six to twelve months. During this period, the patient will undergo a series of exercises and treatments to regain their strength, mobility, and flexibility. The first few weeks after surgery, the physical therapist will focus on reducing the swelling, managing the pain, and gradually increasing the range of motion of the knee. The patient will be taught how to walk with crutches, use a knee brace, and perform some basic exercises like ankle pumps, quad sets, and straight leg raises. As the patient progresses, the physical therapist will introduce more challenging exercises and activities such as cycling, swimming, and weight-bearing exercises. The patient will also be taught how to perform functional movements like squatting, jumping, and landing to prepare them for their daily activities or sports.

Pros and Cons of Physical Therapy After ACL Surgery

Pros:
  • Speeds up the recovery process
  • Reduces pain and swelling
  • Improves strength, mobility, and flexibility
  • Prevents future injuries
  • Helps to regain confidence and independence
Cons:
  • Can be expensive if not covered by insurance
  • Requires a lot of time and effort
  • Can be physically and emotionally challenging
  • May not guarantee a full recovery

FAQs about Physical Therapy After ACL Surgery

Q: Is physical therapy necessary after ACL surgery?
A: Yes, physical therapy is necessary after ACL surgery to ensure a full and proper recovery. Q: How long does physical therapy last after ACL surgery?
A: Physical therapy after ACL surgery typically lasts for six to twelve months. Q: How often do I need to attend physical therapy sessions?
A: The frequency of physical therapy sessions varies depending on the patient's condition and the recommendation of the physical therapist. It can be twice a week or more, especially during the first few months after surgery. Q: Can I do physical therapy at home?
A: Yes, you can do some physical therapy exercises at home, but it is recommended to have a physical therapist supervise and guide you to ensure that you are doing the exercises correctly. Q: What happens if I skip physical therapy sessions?
A: Skipping physical therapy sessions can delay your recovery and may cause complications such as stiffness, weakness, and re-injury. In conclusion, physical therapy after ACL surgery is a crucial part of the recovery process. It helps to speed up the healing, reduce pain and swelling, improve strength, mobility, and flexibility, prevent future injuries, and regain confidence and independence. The duration of physical therapy after ACL surgery can last for six to twelve months, depending on the patient's condition and the extent of the injury. It is important to follow the recommendation of the physical therapist and attend all the scheduled sessions to ensure a full and proper recovery.
