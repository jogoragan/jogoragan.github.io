As educators and parents, we all want our elementary students to be healthy and active. But, it can be challenging to find ways to keep them engaged and interested in physical activity. That's why we've put together a list of fun health activities for elementary students that will help keep them moving and encourage healthy habits.

Table of Contents

  • Yoga and Stretching
  • Dance Party
  • Obstacle Course
  • Fitness Scavenger Hunt

Yoga and Stretching

Yoga and stretching are great ways to encourage flexibility and mindfulness in elementary students. You can use videos, books, or even create your own yoga sequences to keep things interesting. Start with simple poses and stretches and gradually increase the difficulty as students become more comfortable.

How to:

Find a quiet space with enough room for students to move around comfortably. Pick a few yoga poses or stretches to focus on and demonstrate them for the class. Encourage students to follow along and take deep breaths while stretching. Allow time for students to try poses on their own and offer modifications to make poses more accessible for all students.

Tips:

  • Use visual aids, such as posters or flashcards, to help students remember poses.
  • Encourage students to listen to their bodies and modify poses if they feel uncomfortable.
  • Try incorporating mindfulness exercises, such as guided meditations, into your yoga and stretching sessions.

Pros:

  • Yoga and stretching are low-impact exercises that can improve flexibility and reduce stress.
  • These activities can be done indoors or outdoors and require minimal equipment.
  • Yoga and stretching can be adapted to all skill levels and abilities.

Cons:

  • Some students may not be comfortable with certain poses or stretches.
  • Yoga and stretching may not provide a high-intensity workout for students who prefer more vigorous activities.
  • Students may need reminders to focus on their breathing and practice mindfulness during these activities.

Dance Party

Dancing is a fun and energetic way to get elementary students moving. You can choose a specific type of dance, such as hip hop or salsa, or let students choose their own moves. Play upbeat music and encourage students to dance freely and expressively.

How to:

Clear out some space in the classroom or gym and turn up the music. Encourage students to dance freely and try out different moves. You can also teach basic dance steps and allow students to create their own dance routines. Have fun and join in on the dancing!

Tips:

  • Provide a variety of music genres to appeal to different tastes.
  • Try incorporating dance games, such as freeze dance or musical chairs, to add a competitive element.
  • Encourage students to create their own dance routines and perform them for the class.

Pros:

  • Dancing is a fun and social activity that can boost mood and energy levels.
  • Students can express themselves creatively through dance.
  • Dancing can improve coordination, balance, and cardiovascular health.

Cons:

  • Some students may feel self-conscious or uncomfortable dancing in front of others.
  • Dancing may not be suitable for students with certain physical limitations or injuries.
  • Students may need reminders to stay safe and avoid bumping into others while dancing.

Obstacle Course

An obstacle course is a fun and challenging way to get students moving and improve their agility and coordination. You can create your own course using cones, hula hoops, and other equipment, or use a pre-made course.

How to:

Set up a course with a variety of obstacles, such as cones to weave around, hula hoops to jump through, and balance beams to walk across. Demonstrate each obstacle and have students practice before attempting the full course. Time students as they complete the course and encourage them to beat their own times.

Tips:

  • Adjust the difficulty of the course to match students' abilities.
  • Encourage students to cheer each other on and offer support.
  • Try adding fun challenges, such as crawling under a tunnel or tossing a ball into a basket, to keep things interesting.

Pros:

  • Obstacle courses can improve coordination, balance, and agility.
  • These activities can be done indoors or outdoors and require minimal equipment.
  • Obstacle courses can be adapted to all skill levels and abilities.

Cons:

  • Some students may struggle with certain obstacles and become frustrated.
  • Obstacle courses may not provide a high-intensity workout for students who prefer more vigorous activities.
  • Students may need reminders to stay safe and avoid collisions while completing the course.

Fitness Scavenger Hunt

A fitness scavenger hunt is a fun way to get students moving and encourage them to explore their surroundings. You can create a list of fitness-related items, such as a basketball hoop or a set of stairs, and challenge students to find them.

How to:

Create a list of fitness-related items and give each student a copy. Divide students into small groups and set a time limit for the scavenger hunt. Students must find and take a picture of each item on the list. The first group to find all items on the list wins.

Tips:

  • Include a variety of items to appeal to different interests and abilities.
  • Provide a prize for the winning group.
  • Encourage students to work together and communicate effectively.

Pros:

  • A fitness scavenger hunt can be a fun and engaging way to explore the outdoors.
  • These activities can be adapted to all skill levels and abilities.
  • A scavenger hunt can promote teamwork and communication skills.

Cons:

  • Some students may become competitive and lose sight of the fun and educational aspects of the activity.
  • Students may need reminders to stay safe and follow rules while exploring their surroundings.
  • Not all items on the list may be accessible or safe to find.

Healthy Cooking

Cooking healthy meals and snacks can be a fun way to teach students about nutrition and encourage healthy eating habits. You can choose simple recipes that students can make themselves or demonstrate more complex recipes.

How to:

Choose a healthy recipe and gather the necessary ingredients and equipment. Demonstrate each step of the recipe and allow students to try it themselves. Encourage students to try new foods and experiment with different flavors.

Tips:

  • Choose recipes that are easy and fun to make, such as fruit kabobs or homemade granola bars.
  • Include a lesson on nutrition and the benefits of eating a balanced diet.
  • Encourage students to bring their own healthy recipes from home to share with the class.

Pros:

  • Cooking can be a fun and educational way to teach students about nutrition and healthy eating habits.
  • Students can develop cooking skills and learn to make healthy meals and snacks on their own.
  • Cooking can promote teamwork and communication skills.

Cons:

  • Some students may have dietary restrictions or preferences that make it difficult to participate in certain recipes.
  • Cooking activities may require more time and resources than other physical activities.
  • Students may need reminders to stay safe and follow proper food handling and preparation guidelines.

Overall, there are many fun and engaging health activities for elementary students to enjoy. By incorporating physical activity and healthy habits into their daily routines, we can help students develop lifelong skills and promote a healthy lifestyle.
