What is 17 Day Diet?

17 Day Diet is a popular weight loss program that was created by Dr. Mike Moreno. The program consists of four cycles, each lasting 17 days, that focus on different aspects of weight loss such as reducing inflammation, resetting metabolism, and improving digestive health. Cycle 1 is the most restrictive phase of the program, and it is designed to jumpstart your weight loss by eliminating sugar, processed foods, and most carbohydrates.

Cycle 1 Meal Plan

The meal plan for Cycle 1 is based on a low-carbohydrate, high-protein, and low-fat diet. You are allowed to have three meals and two snacks per day, and you should aim to consume between 1,200 and 1,500 calories per day. Here is a sample meal plan for Cycle 1:

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt with berries and a handful of almonds
  • Snack: Hard-boiled egg and carrot sticks
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken breast with steamed vegetables
  • Snack: Apple slices with almond butter
  • Dinner: Baked salmon with roasted asparagus and brown rice

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you succeed on the 17 Day Diet:

  • Plan your meals and snacks ahead of time
  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day
  • Get enough sleep and exercise regularly
  • Avoid processed foods and sugary drinks
  • Use herbs and spices to add flavor to your meals

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the 17 Day Diet:

Can I drink alcohol on the diet?

Alcohol is not recommended during Cycle 1, but you can have a glass of wine or a light beer in moderation during later cycles.

Can I eat fruit on the diet?

Most fruits are not allowed during Cycle 1, but you can have berries in moderation. Other fruits are allowed in later cycles.

Can I eat dairy on the diet?

Low-fat dairy products such as Greek yogurt and cottage cheese are allowed in moderation.

Pros and Cons

Here are some pros and cons of the 17 Day Diet:

Pros

  • Encourages healthy eating habits
  • Provides structure and guidance
  • Includes exercise recommendations
  • Can lead to rapid weight loss

Cons

  • May be too restrictive for some people
  • Requires meal planning and preparation
  • May be difficult to maintain long-term
  • May not be suitable for certain medical conditions
