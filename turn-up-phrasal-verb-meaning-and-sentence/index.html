Meaning of Turn Up Phrasal Verb

The phrasal verb "turn up" means to arrive or appear unexpectedly, or to increase in volume or intensity. It can also mean to find or discover something that was previously lost or missing.

Example Sentences

1. I was so surprised when my old friend turned up at my doorstep after all these years.

2. The music was turned up loud, and we all started dancing.

3. I turned up my collar to shield myself from the cold wind.

4. I can't find my keys anywhere, I hope they turn up soon.

How to Use Turn Up Phrasal Verb

The phrasal verb "turn up" can be used in different contexts. Here are some examples:

1. To arrive unexpectedly

You can use "turn up" when someone arrives unexpectedly or without warning.

Example: I was waiting for my friend at the coffee shop, and she turned up out of nowhere.

2. To increase in volume or intensity

You can use "turn up" when you want to make something louder or more intense.

Example: Can you turn up the music? I can't hear it very well.

3. To find or discover something

You can use "turn up" when you find or discover something that was previously lost or missing.

Example: I lost my phone yesterday, but it turned up in the couch cushions this morning.
