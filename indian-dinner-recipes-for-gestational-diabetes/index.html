Gestational diabetes is a type of diabetes that affects pregnant women. It causes high blood sugar levels and can lead to complications for both the mother and the baby. One way to manage gestational diabetes is by following a healthy diet. In this article, we'll share some Indian dinner recipes that are suitable for women with gestational diabetes. These recipes are not only healthy but also delicious, making it easier to stick to a healthy eating plan.

Recipe 1: Chana Masala

Description: Chana Masala is a popular Indian dish made with chickpeas and spices. It's high in protein and fiber, making it a great option for women with gestational diabetes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of oil

How to:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until onions are translucent.
  2. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Add chickpeas and 1/2 cup of water. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve with brown rice or whole wheat roti.

Tips: You can add spinach or other vegetables to this dish to make it even healthier.

Recipe 2: Palak Paneer

Description: Palak Paneer is a popular vegetarian dish made with spinach and paneer (Indian cheese). It's high in protein, calcium, and iron, making it a great option for women with gestational diabetes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of spinach, washed and chopped
  • 200g of paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until onions are translucent.
  2. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add spinach and cook until wilted.
  4. Add paneer and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve with brown rice or whole wheat roti.

Tips: You can substitute paneer with tofu if you prefer a vegan option.

Recipe 3: Dal Makhani

Description: Dal Makhani is a popular Punjabi dish made with black lentils and kidney beans. It's high in protein and fiber, making it a great option for women with gestational diabetes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of black lentils, soaked overnight
  • 1/2 cup of kidney beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to:

  1. Pressure cook black lentils and kidney beans until tender.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until onions are translucent.
  3. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Add cooked lentils and beans. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve with brown rice or whole wheat roti.

Tips: You can add cream or coconut milk to this dish to make it creamier.

Recipe 4: Tandoori Chicken

Description: Tandoori Chicken is a popular Indian dish made with marinated chicken and spices. It's high in protein and low in fat, making it a great option for women with gestational diabetes.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken breasts, skinless
  • 1 cup of plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to:

  1. Mix yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala in a bowl.
  2. Add chicken breasts to the bowl and coat with the marinade. Marinate for at least 2 hours.
  3. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
  4. Place chicken on a baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until cooked through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve with mixed vegetable curry and brown rice.

Tips: You can grill the chicken instead of baking it for a smokier flavor.
