Simple Exercise To Relieve Lower Back Pain

Written by Alvine Jan 31, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are experiencing lower back pain, you are not alone. Many people suffer from lower back pain due to various reasons such as poor posture, sitting for long hours, or lifting heavy objects. Fortunately, there are simple exercises that you can do to relieve lower back pain. In this article, we will discuss some of the best exercises to help you alleviate your lower back pain.

How to Relieve Lower Back Pain with Exercise

Before we dive into the specific exercises, it is important to understand the benefits of exercise for lower back pain. Exercise helps to strengthen the muscles in your lower back and improve your posture. A combination of stretching and strengthening exercises can help to reduce your pain and prevent it from coming back. Here are some simple exercises that you can do to relieve lower back pain:

Step by Step Guide

1. Cat-Cow Stretch

The cat-cow stretch is a gentle exercise that helps to mobilize your spine and relieve tension in your lower back. To do this exercise, follow these steps:

  1. Start on your hands and knees with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips.
  2. Take a deep breath in and arch your back, lifting your head and tailbone towards the ceiling. This is the cow pose.
  3. As you exhale, round your spine and tuck your chin towards your chest. This is the cat pose.
  4. Repeat this movement, inhaling into cow pose and exhaling into cat pose for 10 repetitions.

2. Child’s Pose

The child’s pose is a restorative yoga pose that helps to stretch your lower back and hips. To do this exercise, follow these steps:

  1. Start on your hands and knees with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips.
  2. Lower your hips back towards your heels and stretch your arms forward.
  3. Rest your forehead on the ground and breathe deeply for 5-10 breaths.

3. Pelvic Tilt

The pelvic tilt is a simple exercise that helps to strengthen your lower back muscles. To do this exercise, follow these steps:

  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground.
  2. Take a deep breath in and as you exhale, gently press your lower back into the ground.
  3. Hold for 5 seconds and release.
  4. Repeat for 10 repetitions.

Tips to Keep in Mind

While these exercises can be very helpful for relieving lower back pain, it is important to keep a few things in mind:

  • Start slowly and gently. Do not push yourself too hard or you may exacerbate your pain.
  • If you feel any sharp or shooting pain, stop the exercise immediately.
  • Consult with your doctor or physical therapist before starting any exercise program.
  • Be consistent with your exercise routine. It may take some time to see results, but regular exercise can help to prevent future episodes of back pain.

Solution to Lower Back Pain

Lower back pain can be a frustrating and debilitating condition, but it is not something that you have to live with. By incorporating simple exercises into your daily routine, you can relieve your pain and prevent it from coming back. Remember to start slowly, be consistent, and consult with your doctor or physical therapist if you have any concerns. With time and persistence, you can overcome your lower back pain and live a pain-free life.

FAQs

Q: Can exercise really help to relieve lower back pain?

A: Yes, exercise can be very effective for relieving lower back pain. Exercise helps to strengthen the muscles in your lower back and improve your posture, which can help to reduce your pain and prevent it from coming back.

Q: How often should I do these exercises?

A: It is recommended to do these exercises at least 2-3 times per week. Be consistent with your exercise routine to see the best results.

Q: Are there any exercises that I should avoid if I have lower back pain?

A: You should avoid exercises that involve twisting, bending forward, or lifting heavy weights. These movements can put stress on your lower back and exacerbate your pain.

Pros and Cons of Exercise for Lower Back Pain

Pros:

  • Exercise helps to strengthen the muscles in your lower back and improve your posture.
  • A combination of stretching and strengthening exercises can help to reduce your pain and prevent it from coming back.
  • Exercise is a natural and non-invasive way to treat lower back pain.

Cons:

  • It may take some time to see results from exercise, so it requires patience and persistence.
  • You should avoid certain exercises if you have lower back pain, which can limit your exercise options.
  • If you have a severe or chronic condition, exercise alone may not be enough to relieve your pain.

In conclusion, exercise can be a very effective way to relieve lower back pain. By incorporating simple exercises into your daily routine, you can strengthen your muscles, improve your posture, and reduce your pain. Remember to start slowly, be consistent, and consult with your doctor or physical therapist if you have any concerns. With time and persistence, you can overcome your lower back pain and live a pain-free life.
