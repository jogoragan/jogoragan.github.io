If you're planning a trip to the Music City, you're in for a treat. Nashville is a hub of culture, food, and entertainment. There's something for everyone, whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or a live music fanatic. Here are 10 amazing day things to do in Nashville in 2023.

1. Explore Nashville's History

Nashville is rich in history, and there are plenty of ways to explore it. Visit the Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum, which houses artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of country music. Take a tour of the Ryman Auditorium, the "Mother Church of Country Music," where legends like Johnny Cash and Dolly Parton have performed. The Tennessee State Capitol is another must-see, with its stunning architecture and rich history.

2. Indulge in Delicious Nashville Food

Nashville is known for its food, and there's no shortage of amazing restaurants to try. Hot chicken is a Nashville specialty, and you can find it at places like Prince's Hot Chicken Shack and Hattie B's. For barbecue, head to Martin's Bar-B-Que Joint, Peg Leg Porker, or Edley's Bar-B-Que. For a taste of Nashville's craft beer scene, check out breweries like Yazoo Brewing Company and Jackalope Brewing Company.

3. Discover Nashville's Art Scene

Nashville's art scene is thriving, with galleries, museums, and public art installations throughout the city. The Frist Art Museum is a must-visit, with its ever-changing exhibits and stunning architecture. The Cheekwood Estate and Gardens is another gem, with its extensive art collection and beautiful botanical gardens. For a taste of Nashville's street art, take a stroll through the Gulch neighborhood, which is filled with colorful murals.

4. Experience Nashville's Live Music Scene

Nashville is the Music City, and live music can be found on every corner. The Grand Ole Opry is a Nashville institution, with its weekly radio show featuring the biggest names in country music. The Bluebird Cafe is another must-visit, with its intimate setting and legendary singer-songwriters. For a taste of Nashville's rock scene, check out venues like the Ryman Auditorium and the Exit/In.

5. Take a Stroll in Nashville's Parks and Nature Areas

Nashville is home to several beautiful parks and nature areas, perfect for a peaceful afternoon stroll. Centennial Park is a popular destination, with its iconic Parthenon replica and beautiful gardens. The Shelby Bottoms Nature Center and Greenway is another gem, with its miles of hiking and biking trails. For a taste of nature without leaving the city, head to the Nashville Zoo, which is home to over 2,700 animals.

6. Go Shopping in Nashville

Nashville has a thriving retail scene, with plenty of boutiques, specialty shops, and malls to explore. The 12 South neighborhood is a popular destination, with its trendy shops and restaurants. The Mall at Green Hills is another must-visit, with its high-end retailers like Louis Vuitton and Burberry. For a taste of Nashville's vintage scene, head to the East Nashville neighborhood, which is filled with thrift stores and antique shops.

7. Catch a Nashville Sports Game

Nashville is home to several sports teams, including the Tennessee Titans (NFL), Nashville Predators (NHL), and Nashville SC (MLS). Catching a game is a great way to experience the city's sports culture and cheer on the home team. The Bridgestone Arena, home of the Predators, is a state-of-the-art facility that hosts concerts and other events in addition to hockey games.

8. Enjoy Family-Friendly Activities in Nashville

Nashville is a great destination for families, with plenty of activities that kids (and parents) will love. The Adventure Science Center is a must-visit, with its interactive exhibits and planetarium. The Nashville Children's Theatre is another great option, with its kid-friendly shows and workshops. For a taste of Nashville history, head to the Belle Meade Plantation, which offers tours and activities for all ages.

9. Experience Nashville's Nightlife

Nashville's nightlife is legendary, with plenty of bars, clubs, and music venues to explore. Broadway is the epicenter of Nashville's nightlife scene, with its honky-tonk bars and live music venues. The Station Inn is another must-visit, with its bluegrass and acoustic music shows. For a taste of Nashville's craft cocktail scene, head to The Patterson House or The Catbird Seat.

10. Other Fun Things to Do in Nashville

There are plenty of other fun things to do in Nashville, depending on your interests. The Lane Motor Museum is a great option for car enthusiasts, with its collection of unique and rare vehicles. The Nashville Farmers' Market is another must-visit, with its local produce, artisanal foods, and craft vendors. For a taste of Nashville's history and architecture, take a walking tour of the city's historic neighborhoods.

No matter what you're interested in, Nashville has something to offer. Plan your trip today and experience all the amazing day things to do in Nashville in 2023!
