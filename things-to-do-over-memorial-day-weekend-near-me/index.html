Things To Do Over Memorial Day Weekend Near Me

Written by Jordan May 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Memorial Day weekend is a time to remember and honor the brave men and women who have sacrificed their lives for our country. It’s also a time to enjoy the beginning of summer with family and friends. If you’re wondering what to do over Memorial Day weekend near me, there are plenty of events and activities to choose from. Here are some of the best options:

1. Attend a Memorial Day Parade or Ceremony

One of the most meaningful ways to honor our fallen soldiers is by attending a Memorial Day parade or ceremony in your community. Many cities and towns host parades that feature marching bands, floats, and veterans’ groups. These events often end with a wreath-laying ceremony or a moment of silence. Check your local newspaper or town website to find out when and where the nearest parade is happening.

2. Visit a National Park

Memorial Day weekend is the unofficial start of summer, and what better way to kick off the season than by exploring nature? The National Park Service is waiving entrance fees on Memorial Day weekend, so it’s the perfect time to visit a nearby park. Whether you want to hike, bike, or just enjoy a picnic with your family, there’s something for everyone at a national park.

3. Have a Barbecue

No Memorial Day weekend would be complete without a barbecue. Fire up the grill and invite your friends and family over for a day of food, fun, and relaxation. Make sure to have plenty of burgers, hot dogs, and cold beverages on hand. You can also ask everyone to bring a side dish or dessert to share.

4. Go to the Beach

If you live near the coast, Memorial Day weekend is the perfect time to hit the beach. Pack a cooler with snacks and drinks, bring your beach umbrella and towels, and spend the day soaking up the sun and playing in the waves. Just make sure to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated!

5. Attend a Music Festival

Many cities and towns host music festivals on Memorial Day weekend. These events feature live music, food vendors, and other activities. It’s a great way to discover new bands and enjoy the outdoors with friends. Check your local events calendar to find out if there’s a music festival happening near you.

6. Watch a Baseball Game

Nothing says “summer” like a baseball game. Many Major League Baseball teams play on Memorial Day weekend, so it’s a great time to cheer on your favorite team. If you can’t make it to a stadium, you can always watch the game on TV or listen to it on the radio.

7. Take a Road Trip

If you have a few days off work, why not take a road trip? Pack up your car and hit the open road. You can visit national parks, historic sites, or just explore new towns and cities. Just make sure to plan your route in advance and bring plenty of snacks and water.

8. Visit a Museum

If you prefer indoor activities, Memorial Day weekend is a great time to visit a museum. Many museums offer special exhibits or events during the holiday weekend. You can learn about history, art, science, and more. Check your local museum’s website to see what’s happening.

9. Participate in a 5K

If you’re looking for a way to get some exercise and support a good cause, consider participating in a Memorial Day 5K. Many cities and towns host these events to raise money for veterans’ organizations. You can walk, run, or jog the 5K and enjoy the company of other participants.

10. Go Camping

If you want to get away from it all and enjoy the great outdoors, consider going camping. You can pitch a tent, build a campfire, and sleep under the stars. Many campgrounds offer activities like hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Just make sure to bring plenty of bug spray and a warm sleeping bag.

Conclusion

There are plenty of things to do over Memorial Day weekend near me. Whether you prefer outdoor activities like hiking and camping or indoor activities like visiting museums and attending concerts, there’s something for everyone. Just make sure to take some time to honor the brave men and women who have made the ultimate sacrifice for our country.
