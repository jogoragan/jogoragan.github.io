What is Push Pull Legs Workout Plan?

The push pull legs (PPL) workout plan is a weight training program that targets different muscle groups on different days. The workout plan is based on the concept of pushing exercises, pulling exercises, and leg exercises.

The push exercises target the muscles used to push weight away from the body, such as the chest, shoulders, and triceps. The pull exercises target the muscles used to pull weight towards the body, such as the back and biceps. The leg exercises target the muscles in the lower body, such as the quads, hamstrings, and glutes.

How Does Push Pull Legs Build Muscle?

The PPL workout plan is an effective way to build muscle because it targets different muscle groups on different days. By doing this, you give each muscle group time to recover before working it again. This allows for maximum muscle growth and a decrease in the risk of overtraining.

Additionally, the PPL workout plan allows for a variety of exercises to be performed, which helps prevent boredom and keeps the body guessing. The variety of exercises also ensures that all muscle groups are targeted for optimal muscle growth.

Step by Step Guide to Push Pull Legs Workout Plan

Before starting the PPL workout plan, it is important to warm up properly to prevent injury. This can be done by performing 5-10 minutes of light cardio and stretching the muscles to be worked.

Here is a sample PPL workout plan:

Push Day:

  • Bench Press
  • Incline Bench Press
  • Shoulder Press
  • Tricep Extensions
  • Lateral Raises

Pull Day:

  • Deadlifts
  • Lat Pulldowns
  • Seated Rows
  • Bicep Curls
  • Hammer Curls

Leg Day:

  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Leg Press
  • Leg Extensions
  • Leg Curls

Each exercise should be performed for 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps, with 1-2 minutes of rest between sets. The weight used should be challenging but not too heavy, as proper form is essential to prevent injury.

Tips for Successful Push Pull Legs Workout Plan

Here are some tips for a successful PPL workout plan:

  • Gradually increase the weight used to avoid injury and ensure progress.
  • Make sure to get enough rest and recovery time between workouts.
  • Focus on proper form to prevent injury and ensure maximum muscle activation.
  • Stay consistent with the workout plan to see results.

FAQ about Push Pull Legs Build Muscle

What is the best time to do the PPL workout plan?

The best time to do the PPL workout plan is when you have the most energy and can dedicate enough time to complete the workout.

Can the PPL workout plan be done at home?

Yes, the PPL workout plan can be done at home with the use of dumbbells, resistance bands, and bodyweight exercises.

How often should the PPL workout plan be done?

The PPL workout plan can be done 3-4 times per week, with rest days in between each workout.

Cons:

  • May require access to gym equipment
