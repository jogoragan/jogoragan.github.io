What is Push Pull Day Workout Split?

Push pull day workout split is a type of workout routine that focuses on the upper body muscles. The routine is designed to target these muscles in a specific order, alternating between push exercises and pull exercises. Push exercises are those that involve pushing weight away from your body, while pull exercises are those that involve pulling weight towards your body.

The push pull day workout split is typically done in a two-day cycle, with one day focusing on push exercises and the other day focusing on pull exercises. This allows your muscles to recover between workouts, which can lead to faster results and less risk of injury.

How to Do Push Pull Day Workout Split

The push pull day workout split can be done using a variety of exercises, including free weights, machines, and bodyweight exercises. The key is to focus on exercises that target the major muscle groups in your upper body, including your chest, back, shoulders, and arms.

Push Day Exercises

Push day exercises are those that involve pushing weight away from your body. Some common push day exercises include:

  • Bench press
  • Shoulder press
  • Incline bench press
  • Dumbbell flys
  • Tricep extensions
  • Push-ups

Pull Day Exercises

Pull day exercises are those that involve pulling weight towards your body. Some common pull day exercises include:

  • Chin-ups
  • Pull-ups
  • Bent-over rows
  • Lat pull-downs
  • Seated cable rows
  • Barbell curls
  • Hammer curls

Step-by-Step Guide for Push Pull Day Workout Split

Here's a step-by-step guide for doing the push pull day workout split:

  1. Choose your exercises: Pick a variety of push and pull exercises that target your upper body muscles.
  2. Decide on your schedule: Determine how often you want to do the push pull day workout split routine, and plan your workouts accordingly.
  3. Warm up: Always start with a warm-up to get your muscles ready for exercise.
  4. Do your push exercises: Complete your push exercises, focusing on proper form and using weights that challenge you but don't cause injury.
  5. Rest: Allow your muscles to recover between exercises and sets.
  6. Do your pull exercises: Complete your pull exercises, again focusing on proper form and using weights that challenge you but don't cause injury.
  7. Cool down: After your workout, take time to stretch and cool down your muscles to prevent injury and reduce soreness.

Tips for Push Pull Day Workout Split

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your push pull day workout split routine:

  • Start slow: If you're new to the push pull day workout split, start with lighter weights and fewer reps until you build up your strength and endurance.
  • Focus on form: Proper form is key to preventing injury and getting the most out of your workouts. Make sure you're using proper form for each exercise.
  • Rest and recover: Allow your muscles to recover between workouts, and take rest days as needed.
  • Mix it up: Don't be afraid to try new exercises or vary your routine to keep it interesting and challenging.

Solutions to Common Problems with Push Pull Day Workout Split

Here are some common problems people experience with the push pull day workout split, and how to solve them:

  • Plateauing: If you're not seeing results or you've reached a plateau, try changing up your routine by adding new exercises or increasing the weight you're using.
  • Injury: If you experience pain or injury, stop your workout and seek medical attention if needed. Make sure you're using proper form and weights that are appropriate for your fitness level.
  • Boredom: If you're getting bored with your routine, try mixing it up with new exercises or varying your schedule.

FAQ about Push Pull Day Workout Split

What muscles does push pull day workout split target?

The push pull day workout split targets the major muscle groups in your upper body, including your chest, back, shoulders, and arms.

How often should I do push pull day workout split?

It's up to you how often you do the push pull day workout split, but most people do it on a two-day cycle, with one day focusing on push exercises and the other day focusing on pull exercises.

Can I do push pull day workout split at home?

Yes, you can do push pull day workout split at home using free weights, resistance bands, or bodyweight exercises.

Pros and Cons of Push Pull Day Workout Split

Pros

  • Targets major muscle groups in your upper body
  • Allows for muscle recovery between workouts
  • Can lead to faster results
  • Less risk of injury compared to other workout routines

Cons

  • May not target lower body muscles as effectively
  • Can become repetitive if not varied
  • May require more equipment than other workout routines

Overall, the push pull day workout split is a great way to target your upper body muscles and build strength and endurance. By following these tips and guidelines, you can get the most out of your workouts and see results in no time.
